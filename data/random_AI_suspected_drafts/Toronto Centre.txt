The Toronto Centre for Global Leadership in Financial Supervision (Toronto Centre) is an international not-for-profit organization based in Toronto, Canada. It provides capacity-building and training programs for financial sector regulators and supervisors. Toronto Centre focuses on strengthening financial stability, crisis preparedness, and financial inclusion training for authorities in emerging markets and developing economies. Its work supports broader global priorities, including the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals and Canada’s Feminist International Assistance Policy.

Toronto Centre is primarily funded by international development agencies and multilateral institutions. The organization's founding members include the International Monetary Fund, World Bank Group and the Schulich School of Business at York University. Toronto Centre’s current CEO is Babak Abbaszadeh.

## History

Toronto Centre is an independent not-for-profit organization established in 1998. It was founded by the Government of Canada through the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA) and Finance Canada, the World Bank Group, and the Schulich School of Business at York University.

The organization was created in the aftermath of the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis to incrase effective financial regulation and supervision to promote financial stability and prevent future crisis. Its training and programs target financial regulators, supervisors, and authorities responsible for consumer protection and failure resolution in emerging markets and developing economies.

Since its founding, Toronto Centre has trained more than 30,000 central bankers, financial supervisors, and regulators from over 190 jurisdictions.

## Thematic & Regional Areas of Focus

Toronto Centre’s primary focus is the advancement of risk-based supervision as the mechanism to advance and promote financial stability. Toronto Centre programming covers a range of topics and themes including climate risk, financial stability combatting financial crime (AML / CTF / CPF), SupTech and RegTech, and financial inclusion.

Toronto Centre delivers its programs through in-country or virtual engagements held in partnerships with the internal training academies of National Regulators, or hosted with National Regulators for regional participation.

## Implementing Partner to Global Standard Setters

Toronto Centre is a noted parter of Canadian Agencies OSFI and Finance Canada, as well as a partner to International Standard Setters. Toronto Centre operate both bilaterally and multilaterally as demonstrated by their history collaborating with the Financial Action Task Force and its FATF Style Regional Bodies, The International Organization of Securities Commissions (IOSCO), International Association of Insurance Supervisors (IAIS), and the Network for Greening of the Financial System (NGFS). The organization routinely holds events in partnership with the IMF at their Annual Spring and Fall Meetings.
