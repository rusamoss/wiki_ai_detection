Betty H. Greenman is an American International Board Certified Lactation Consultant (IBCLC) and maternal health educator based in Queens, New York. She founded Breastfeeding With Love, a private practice offering in-home lactation consultations in the New York metropolitan area. She has been quoted as a lactation expert in publications including Business Insider, New York Magazine, and Today's Parent.

## Education and credentials

Greenman holds a B.S. in Maternal and Child Health / Lactation from Union Institute and University. She holds the International Board Certified Lactation Consultant (IBCLC) credential, administered by the International Board of Lactation Consultant Examiners (IBLCE).

## Career

Greenman founded Breastfeeding With Love in Queens, New York, a private practice that offers in-home lactation consultations across the New York metropolitan area.

## Media coverage

Greenman has been quoted as a lactation expert in several national and international publications.

In June 2018, Yahoo Lifestyle published an article on actor Tia Mowry 's breastfeeding experience. Greenman was quoted explaining that certain medical conditions—including prematurity, cleft palate, Down syndrome, and metabolic disorders—can make latching and breastfeeding more difficult.

Also in June 2018, Business Insider covered the story of Khloé Kardashian using a formula-mixing machine after struggling to produce enough breast milk. Greenman was quoted explaining that milk supply is driven by infant demand and identified several factors that can reduce supply, including hormonal imbalances, thyroid levels, diabetes, hypertension, prior breast surgery, and certain medications.

In May 2018, The Strategist, a product-recommendation section of New York Magazine, cited Greenman in a roundup of the best nursing bras for breastfeeding and pumping.

In January 2019, Business Insider published an article on breastfeeding and postpartum weight loss, quoting Greenman on the caloric demands of breastfeeding and the risks of low-calorie dieting for milk supply.

In November 2018, Today's Parent (Canada) consulted Greenman for an article on tattoos and breastfeeding. She stated that tattoo ink molecules are too large to pass into breast milk, but advised against new tattoos while nursing due to infection risk from unlicensed tattoo parlors.

In October 2023, The Daily Meal cited Greenman in an article on teas to avoid, noting that sage tea has been linked to decreased milk production in breastfeeding mothers.
