B.E. Mann is an American reggae musician, radio broadcaster, and independent record producer from Akron, Ohio. Active since the early 1990s, he has released recordings independently and has been involved in regional radio broadcasting and educational television production. Coverage in regional and international publications has documented his performance career, broadcasting work, and recordings.

## Early life and background

Mann grew up in Akron, Ohio, where he developed an early interest in music and broadcasting. Regional press reports note that he was introduced to radio production through family involvement in community and religious programming and later became active in reggae performance and sound-system culture.

## Career

### Radio

Alongside his performance work, Mann was active in radio broadcasting. As a teenager he became involved with WAPS-FM in Akron, and in 1988 he launched Reggae Rhythms from Around the World, a weekly program that continued for many years.

### King Ov Heartz and Identity

In the early 1990s Mann founded the reggae band King Ov Heartz, performing across Northeast Ohio.

In 1995 he briefly fronted the Island Records–affiliated reggae group Identity during a period of lineup changes.

### Solo work

Mann later focused on solo recording and production while continuing to perform. Newspaper profiles in Ohio publications described him as a prolific independent reggae artist with audiences extending beyond the region.

Additional reporting in regional newspapers has referenced his live performances and ongoing recording output.

## Television and media

Regional press has documented Mann’s involvement in educational television. Coverage notes his participation in projects connected with Study Jam, a WVIZ-TV instructional series in Cleveland.

Educational materials for the program identify King Ov Heartz as the program’s house band and interview participants.

He was also involved in the Akron Public Schools children’s program Ask Gilby, which received recognition from the Lower Great Lakes Chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences.

## Reception

Mann has been the subject of feature coverage in regional newspapers and music publications over multiple decades. Early reporting in the Akron Beacon Journal documented the formation of King Ov Heartz and the group’s regional performance history.

In 2000, Revue Magazine reported that Mann had achieved chart success in Europe, describing him as “one of the most popular dance artists in the UK.”

A 2005 profile in Germany’s Reggae News Magazine (B.E.Mann Neues Album) discussed his recording output and international audience.

In a 2007 feature, the Sandusky Register reported that Mann had earned six No. 1 songs in eight European countries and had released more than two dozen albums.

Later profiles in The Canton Repository and the Akron Beacon Journal reviewed his retrospective releases and long-running performance career.
