## Henry C. Boyd III

Henry C. Boyd III is an American marketing professor, attorney, and media contributor. He serves as Clinical Professor of Marketing at the Robert H. Smith School of Business at the University of Maryland, College Park.

### Early life and education

Boyd earned an A.B. in Chemistry from Princeton University. He later completed an MBA in Marketing at the University of California, Berkeley Haas School of Business, a Ph.D. in Marketing (Consumer Behavior) from Duke University, and a J.D. in Intellectual Property Law from the University of Wisconsin Law School. He is licensed to practice law in Maryland and Wisconsin, including the U.S. District Court for the Western District of Wisconsin.

### Academic career

At the Smith School, Boyd teaches marketing strategy, consumer behavior, branding, and race and identity in marketing. He has received multiple teaching awards, including the Storyteller Award for Teaching Excellence and “Most Valuable Professor” honors from Maryland Women’s Lacrosse.

In 2005, Boyd co-authored *Consumer Entitlement Theory and Measurement* in *Psychology & Marketing*, introducing the Consumer Entitlement Inventory (CEI), which has become a widely cited tool in consumer behavior research.

### Media presence and commentary

Boyd frequently contributes marketing insights in mainstream and industry media. His work has been featured in *The Washington Post*, *USA Today*, *The Atlantic*, *Slate*, *Sports Illustrated*, and *Crain’s Chicago Business*.

He maintained the “Smith Brain Trust” blog with commentary on popular culture, including articles like *"‘Black Panther’ Isn’t Just a Great Movie, It’s Great Marketing"* and *"2018’s Marketing Hits and Misses."*

He has also been featured for marketing commentary in university publications, such as creative advertising during Volkswagen’s brand redesign and narratives about the Olympics’ resilience in sports marketing.

### Public service and global teaching

During the COVID‑19 pandemic, Boyd and his family produced and donated face shields and hand sanitizer to frontline healthcare workers at Johns Hopkins Hospital.

He has also taught internationally, including lectures in Cape Town, Johannesburg, Beijing, Maputo, Thailand, and France.

### Additional recognition

Boyd serves as a managing director and principal at Ombudsman LLC, a consultancy.[^25] He has worked in industry as a pharmaceutical representative at Merck and an economic forecaster at IBM, and he has consulted for organizations such as the NFL, ExxonMobil, SAIC, Verizon, Stanley Black & Decker, and Ocean Tomo.
