Karso Organization for Youth (ڕیکخراوی کارسۆ بۆ گەنجان; abbreviated KOY) is a non-governmental youth organization founded in 2025, headquartered in Shekhan, Sulaymaniyah, Iraq.

## Overview

The organization’s internal regulations list the organization name in Arabic, Kurdish, and English, and use the abbreviation KOY. The head office address is stated as Iraq, Sulaymaniyah, Shekhan.

## Objectives

The organization aims to provide skills development and capacity-building opportunities for youth to support their effective participation in society. It also aims to reduce youth unemployment by supporting entrepreneurship and small projects and expanding economic opportunities for youth. The objectives include environmental awareness related to climate change impacts, pollution reduction, and promoting recycling and positive environmental behavior.

## Activities

The internal regulations describe activities such as training workshops, events supporting peaceful coexistence, dialogue platforms, and funding-related courses to help youth start projects. They also mention partnerships with organizations to support innovation, environmental awareness campaigns, environmentally focused projects, and cultural festivals and workshops.

## Governance

The organizational structure includes a founding body, a general assembly, and an administrative board. The administrative board is described as having six members appointed by the founding body, with defined powers for the board president and vice president.

## Funding

The regulations list funding sources including annual membership fees (50,000 IQD), donations/grants, and income from the organization’s activities and projects.

## Branches

The regulations state that the organization may open branches inside and outside Iraq, by decision of the administrative board through a simple majority vote.
