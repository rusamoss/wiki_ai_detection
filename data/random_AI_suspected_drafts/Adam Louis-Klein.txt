Adam Louis-Klein is an American anthropologist and writer. He is a PhD candidate in anthropology at McGill University and a Postgraduate Fellow at the London Centre for the Study of Contemporary Antisemitism. He has written and spoken publicly about antisemitism and anti-Zionism, including in interviews and hosted media programs. In 2025, he launched the Movement Against Antizionism (MAAZ). His academic research has included fieldwork with the Desana people in the Vaupés region of the Northwest Amazon.

## Early life and education

Louis-Klein earned a B.A. in philosophy from Yale University and an M.A. in philosophy from The New School for Social Research. He later pursued anthropology at the University of Chicago.

## Academic work

Louis-Klein is pursuing doctoral study in anthropology at McGill University. In public interviews, he has described fieldwork with the Desana in the Northwest Amazon and has discussed comparative questions of indigeneity, historical narrative, and peoplehood.

The London Centre for the Study of Contemporary Antisemitism describes his work as bridging symbolic anthropology, Jewish thought, and political theory, and characterizes his public writing as engaging anti-Zionism, Jewish indigeneity, and “civilizational continuity.”

## Approach

In interviews and institutional biographies, Louis-Klein has described his research on Indigenous cosmology in the Northwest Amazon and his public writing on antisemitism and anti-Zionism as connected through broader questions of peoplehood, indigeneity, and historical narrative.

## Movement Against Antizionism

In 2025, Louis-Klein launched the Movement Against Antizionism (MAAZ), which argues that anti-Zionism functions as a form of antisemitism and seeks to promote public education on the issue. Coverage of the initiative reported plans for an inaugural conference in Pittsburgh in summer 2026. A January 2026 interview in Quillette described Louis-Klein as having founded MAAZ.

## Research and views

Louis-Klein has argued in public commentary and interviews that contemporary anti-Zionism should be understood as a form of antisemitism rather than solely as a political position on Israel. A December 2025 report in The Times of Israel on activist academics described this position as treating anti-Zionism as “a new chapter of Jew-hatred.”

As a fellow at the London Centre for the Study of Contemporary Antisemitism, he has also written methodological commentary on how antisemitism is analyzed and discussed.

## Public engagement

Louis-Klein has discussed his work in interviews, podcasts, and seminars. In August 2025, he appeared on the American Jewish Committee podcast People of the Pod, where he discussed parallels between Indigenous identity and Jewish peoplehood and addressed antisemitism in academic contexts.

He participated in the London Centre for the Study of Contemporary Antisemitism’s 2025 Autumn Seminar Series, including a seminar on anti-Zionism and critical reasoning. A recording of one such seminar was published on the Centre’s YouTube channel.

## Oscillations

Louis-Klein has collaborated with anthropologist Justin Shaffner on Oscillations: Non-Standard Experiments in Anthropology, the Social Sciences, and Cosmology, an editorial platform for long-form and experimental work. A co-authored project statement describing Oscillations was published on Academia.edu.

A Royal Holloway research-portal record lists Louis-Klein among the editors of an Oscillations publication. A 2021 post by philosopher Edward P. Butler also thanked the editors of Oscillations, naming Louis-Klein and Shaffner.

## Sky–Earth System

In collaborative work with Shaffner, Louis-Klein has outlined a framework they call the “Sky–Earth System,” described in a co-authored manifesto published on Academia.edu. The manifesto has also been discussed in hosted audio formats, including an episode description published by the War Machine podcast.
