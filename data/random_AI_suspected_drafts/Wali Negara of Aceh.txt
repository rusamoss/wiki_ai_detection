The Wali Negara of Aceh (Acehnese: Wali Neugara Aceh) as a political office in the history of the Sultanate of Aceh that referred to the holder of the highest governmental authority who exercised state functions on behalf of the sultan under certain circumstances, such as when the sultan was a minor, is unable to rule, or during political and military crises, as well as in other circumstances such as the sultan’s arrest or capture by the enemy during war; in such cases, the Wali Negara automatically assumes the highest position in all matters of governance, including political and military affairs. In this role, the Wali Negara acts as the administrator of state affairs, a strategic decision-maker, and a guardian of political stability; thus, functionally, his position can be equated with that of a “Head of State” in a monarchy—that is, a temporary ruler who governs on behalf of the legitimate sovereign.

## Etymology

The term Wali Negara in the context of Acehnese history is more appropriately understood in English as “Head of State,” while Wali Nanggroë is better translated as “Guardian.” Although the two terms may appear similar, they differ significantly in meaning. This distinction is comparable to the difference between the English words “country” and “county,” which are similar in form but represent entirely different concepts.

The term Wali Nanggroë differs in meaning from Wali Negara, both conceptually and in its contextual usage. Wali Nanggroë is not associated with the framework of a modern state; rather, it refers to a symbolic authority rooted in Acehnese adat and cultural tradition. Consequently, the Acehnese word nanggroë is not an appropriate translation for “state,” as the correct term for a political state in Acehnese is neugara. Instead, nanggroë is better understood as “ negeri,” which can be translated into English as “ land ” or “ realm,” denoting a broader cultural and historical territory that is not necessarily tied to the concept of a sovereign state, but rather reflects the collective identity, living space, and unity of the Acehnese people.

## History of the Wali Negara

In the Law of the Aceh Kingdom (Qanun Meukuta Alam Al-Asyi), it is stated that the authority of the Sultan is equal to that of the Malikkul Adil and the Head of Reusam. The highest authority resides in the Parliamentary Council. This council is the body that grants rights and obligations and holds full power over customs and laws. Therefore, when Sultan Mahmud Syah II passed away on January 25, 1874, those who remained were Malikul Adil Teungku Imum Lueng Bata and the customary leader Teungku Chik di Tiro Muhammad Saman.

At the time when war was raging in Bandar Aceh, all members of parliament, customary leaders, the acting Sultan (since Sultan Muhammad Daud Syah II was only 11 years old at the time), and the Malikul Adil migrated to the land of Pedir (Pidie) as part of a war strategy. After three days of travel, on January 28, 1874, they arrived in Keumala, in the land of Pedir, and the parliament immediately withdrew all customary and legal authority into the hands of the parliament. The members of parliament at that time were Tuanku Raja Keumala, Tuanku Banta Hasyem, Teuku Panglima Polem, and Teungku Chik di Tanoh Abee Syeh Abdulwahab. At that moment, Tuanku Raja Keumala, before the Assembly, proclaimed the transfer of the authority of the Aceh Kingdom to Teungku Chik di Tiro Muhammad Saman on January 28, 1874.

From that point onward, it was legitimately established that Teungku Chik di Tiro Muhammad Saman became the person in charge and held full authority in the Aceh state as Mudabbirul Muluk, namely the First Wali Negara of Aceh. Teungku Chik di Tiro Muhammad Saman led the Aceh state for 17 years and was martyred on January 21, 1891. Subsequently, the authority and struggle of the Aceh state were continued by his eldest son, Teungku Muhammad Amin bin Muhammad Saman (martyred 1896), followed by Teungku Abdussalam bin Muhammad Saman Tiro (martyred 1898), Teungku Sulaiman bin Muhammad Saman Tiro (martyred 1902), Teungku Ubaidillah bin Muhammad Saman Tiro (martyred 1905), Teungku Mahyiddin bin Muhammad Saman Tiro (martyred 1910), and Teungku Mu’az bin Muhammad Amin Tiro (martyred December 3, 1911).

The struggle of the Acehnese people continued until the Dutch East Indies succeeded in conquering Aceh and dissolving the Aceh Sultanate. Then, after 65 years (counted from the martyrdom of Teungku Mu’az on December 3, 1911), the struggle experienced a vacuum. Precisely on December 4, 1976, Tengku Hasan di Tiro proclaimed again the independence of Aceh as a continuation of the struggle, the “Successor of State” of the Aceh Kingdom in the past. From historical facts, the institution of Wali Negara began with the appointment of Teungku Chik di Tiro Muhammad Saman as the person responsible and fully in power in the Aceh state as Mudabbirul Muluk, namely the legitimate Wali Negara of Aceh on January 28, 1874.

The title of Wali Negara was also once held by Teungku Daud Beureueh when he established the Darul Islam movement in Aceh, which later continued with the establishment of the Islamic Republic of Aceh (RIA). However, the succession of Wali Negara after his passing did not continue. A similar situation occurred after Teungku Hasan di Tiro passed away; no one, whether from the di Tiro family lineage or not, has claimed himself as the successor of the Wali Negara. In the book “Acheh – New Birth of Freedom” by Tengku Hasan di Tiro, published by the British Parliament House of Lords on May 1, 1992, in Appendix II, the name Teungku Hasan di Tiro is listed as the 41st ruler of Aceh, beginning from Sultan Ali Mughayat Syah (1500–1530) up to Tengku Hasan di Tiro (1976–2010).

The title of Wali Negara is temporary in nature, until Aceh is free (bibeuëh) from the grip of foreign colonial domination. When it later becomes independent and sovereign, the holder of the title will hand over the affairs of the Acehnese state to its own people.

The condition of modern Aceh after the implementation of the Helsinki MoU has resulted in the title of Wali Negara, which previously stood independently, being repositioned. However, this change does not eliminate the position of the Wali Negara of Aceh. Rather, this transition represents the continuity of Aceh as a region capable of managing its own affairs, as well as preserving its customary traditions. Officially, by the Government of Indonesia, the position of Wali Negara, which was previously temporary, has now been formalized into law and made a permanent position in accordance with the mandate of Law Number 11 of 2006. Under this law, Aceh is granted special autonomy, one aspect of which is the legal and formal recognition of the position of Wali Negara.

## Public Response

In political dynamics, information that is repeatedly circulated in everyday life—even if incorrect—can become accepted as truth, even when recorded as part of history. So far, there have been efforts by certain parties to distort history based on political interests. To equate the former Wali Negara with the present Wali Nanggroë, those parties must seek permission from the family of the Sultan who originally granted mandates to the previous guardians, in the sense of representing a national entity.

Due to the absence of a historical connection with the past, the current position of Wali Nanggroë should properly be referred to as the 1st (first) Wali Nanggroë, not the 9th (ninth). This is because it is not a Wali for the sovereignty of the Acehnese nation. Therefore, there is no relationship between Wali Negara and Wali Nanggroë established after the Helsinki MoU, under which Aceh remains within Indonesia.
