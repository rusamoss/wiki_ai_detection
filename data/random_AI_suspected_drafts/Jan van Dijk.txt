Jan J. M. van Dijk (born 1947) is a Dutch criminologist, victimologist, and professor of law. He is widely recognized as one of the pioneers of modern victimology and a key initiator of the International Crime Victims Survey (ICVS). In 2012, he was awarded the Stockholm Prize in Criminology, often referred to as the "Nobel Prize in Criminology".

## Biography and career

Jan van Dijk graduated with a law degree from Leiden University in 1970. In 1977, he obtained his Ph.D. in criminology from Radboud University Nijmegen.

Throughout his career, he has combined academic work with roles in government and international organizations. For many years, he served as a policy director and the Director of the Research and Documentation Centre (WODC) at the Dutch Ministry of Justice and Security.

In 1998, Van Dijk joined the United Nations as the officer in charge of the crime prevention program of the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) in Vienna. He has also served as a member of the Group of Experts on Action against Trafficking in Human Beings (GRETA) at the Council of Europe.

In academia, van Dijk taught as a professor of criminology at Leiden University. He subsequently held the Pieter van Vollenhoven Chair in Victimology and Human Security at the International Victimology Institute (INTERVICT) at Tilburg University.

## Scientific contributions

Van Dijk's primary research focus is victimology. He consistently opposed the traditional, marginal treatment of victims in the criminal justice system and statistical research, advocating for bringing them to the center of European criminology.

In 1987, he initiated the International Crime Victims Survey (ICVS). This was a groundbreaking research project that, instead of relying on official police statistics (which are often distorted by legal differences and a reluctance to report crimes), collected survey data directly from citizens. He supervised the survey's implementation across more than 80 countries, providing a reliable tool for comparing crime rates and victimization globally.

## Awards and honors

For his fundamental contributions to penal sciences, van Dijk has been honored with several international awards:

* 2009: Sellin-Glueck Award, presented by the American Society of Criminology for outstanding contributions to international criminology.
* 2012: Stockholm Prize in Criminology for his long-term leadership of the ICVS project.
* State honors: Officer of the Order of Orange-Nassau, a Dutch civil and military order of chivalry.
