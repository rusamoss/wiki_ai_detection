Aker Scholarship is a Norwegian scholarship program that provides funding and guidance for students from Norway to pursue master's or doctoral degrees at world leading universities. It is administered and awarded by Anne Grete Eidsvig and Kjell Inge Røkke's Charitable Foundation for Education (Anne Grete Eidsvig og Kjell Inge Røkkes allmennyttige stiftelse for utdanning).

## History

The Aker Scholarship was established in 2015 by Anne Grete Eidsvig and Kjell Inge Røkke's Charitable Foundation for Education (Anne Grete Eidsvig og Kjell Inge Røkkes allmennyttige stiftelse for utdanning) in cooperation with Aker ASA. Its purpose is to enable highly qualified students with ties to Norway to pursue master's or doctoral degrees at leading international universities while fostering a commitment to contribute to Norwegian society.

Between 20 and 30 students receive the Aker Scholarship each year. The first group of scholars began their studies in 2016, and by the end of 2025 the foundation reported having supported 239 students across a range of disciplines.

## Eligibility and selection

Applicants must have a strong connection to Norway (by nationality or long-term residence) and scholarships are open to all academic fields and to both master's and PhD programs.

The deadline for application is in September and interviews are conducted in early October. After the interviews the most promising candidates are given conditional scholarship, as well as thorough guidance through the universities’ application processes. The condition of the scholarship is acceptance at one of the nine collaboration universities.

### Administration

The day-to-day operations of the foundation are managed by an administrative team based in Oslo and is led by Bjørn Blindheim.

## Aker Scholars

The recipients of the scholarship are called Aker Scholars. The students become part of an active community with frequent academic and social gatherings, where they get to know each other across cohorts and fields of study.
