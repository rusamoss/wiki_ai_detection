Professor Dr. Suchana Chavanich (also known as Apple Chavanich) is a Thai marine scientist. She is recognized as the first Thai female scientist to conduct research in Antarctica. She is currently a professor in the Department of Marine Science, Faculty of Science, Chulalongkorn University. She is also a member of the Thai research team that successfully achieved Thailand’s first sexual propagation of corals.

## Early Life and Education

Suchana Chavanich completed her secondary education at Chulalongkorn University Demonstration School. She then earned her bachelor's degree in Marine Science from the Faculty of Science, Chulalongkorn University, Bangkok, Thailand. She pursued graduate studies in the United States, obtaining a Master’s degree from Central Connecticut State University and a Ph.D. from the University of New Hampshire.

In 2021, she was appointed Professor in Oceanography at Chulalongkorn University.

## Antarctic Research

One of Chavanich’s most notable achievements is becoming the first Thai female scientist selected to participate in Antarctic research expeditions. In 2009, she joined the 51st Japanese Antarctic Research Expedition (JARE-51) to study the impacts of climate change on living organisms and the environment. In 2014, she participated in the 30th Chinese National Antarctic Research Expedition (CHINARE 30), traveling to the Antarctic Peninsula under the Polar Research Program initiated by Her Royal Highness Princess Maha Chakri Sirindhorn of Thailand. The mission focused on studying the effects of climate change on ecosystems and the environment.

During this expedition, she conducted SCUBA diving in Antarctic waters, becoming the first Thai female scientist to dive in the Antarctic Ocean.

## Academic and Professional Work

In addition to her academic role, Chavanich is a certified scuba diving instructor, guest lecturer, academic advisor, writer, and translator. She has contributed articles related to diving, marine conservation, and marine tourism to magazines, universities, and government agencies both in Thailand and internationally. Her research focuses on marine ecosystems and marine resource conservation in both polar and tropical regions.

## Awards and Recognition

Professor Chavanich has received numerous awards and honors, including:

* Chevalier dans l’Ordre des Palmes académiques (Knight of the Order of Academic Palms), awarded by the Government of the French Republic (2024)
* ASEAN Biodiversity Heroes Award, presented by the ASEAN Centre for Biodiversity (2022)

* Explorer Award, National Geographic Magazine Thailand (2018)
* Selected as one of the “17 Asia Power Women of Inspiration” by Her World magazine (2015)

* Outstanding Scientist Award, UNESCO–IOC/WESTPAC (2014)

* Selected as one of the “100 Most Inspiring People in Thailand” by The In Residence (2013)
* For Women in Science Award (Life Sciences), awarded by L’Oréal Thailand in collaboration with UNESCO (2008)
