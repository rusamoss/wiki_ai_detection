Marina Chiara Garassino is an Italian medical oncologist specialising in thoracic malignancies, including lung cancer, non–small cell lung cancer (NSCLC), small cell lung cancer (SCLC), mesothelioma, and thymic tumours. She is Professor of Medicine and Director of Thoracic Oncology Programs at the University of Chicago Medicine.

She has been involved in international clinical research on targeted therapies and immunotherapies for thoracic cancers, and has authored more than 200 peer-reviewed publications in leading medical journals. Her work, including her advocacy for gender equity in oncology leadership, has been discussed in Italian national media such as Corriere della Sera.

## Early life and education

Garassino received her medical degree from the University of Milan in 1995, graduating cum laude. She completed her specialisation in medical oncology at the University of Milan in 2000. In 2009 she was awarded a fellowship by the European Society for Medical Oncology (ESMO), during which she trained at The Christie NHS Foundation Trust in Manchester, United Kingdom.

She later completed a master's degree in management in oncology at Bocconi University in Milan.

## Academic career

Garassino served as head of the Thoracic Medical Oncology Unit at the Fondazione IRCCS Istituto Nazionale dei Tumori in Milan, one of Italy’s leading cancer research centres.

Between 2017 and 2021 she held a position as contract professor of medicine and surgery at the University of Milan. In 2021 she joined the University of Chicago Medicine as Professor of Medicine and Director of Thoracic Oncology Programs.

## Research

Garassino’s research focuses on biomarker-driven therapies, molecularly targeted treatments, and immune checkpoint inhibitors in thoracic oncology. She has served as a principal investigator and member of steering committees for several international clinical trials in lung cancer.

Her publications appear in major peer-reviewed journals including The New England Journal of Medicine, The Lancet Oncology, Journal of Clinical Oncology, and Annals of Oncology.

## Professional service

Garassino has held leadership roles within international oncology organisations. She served as Italy’s national representative to the European Society for Medical Oncology from 2011 to 2019 and later became chair of the ESMO National Societies Committee.

She is the founder and president of Women for Oncology Italy, an initiative aimed at supporting leadership development and collaboration among women working in oncology.
