Waman Sudama Nimbalkar (Marathi: वामन सुदामा निंबाळकर; 13 March 1943 – 3 December 2010) was a Marathi Dalit poet, literary critic, publisher, and activist from Maharashtra, India. One of the foundational figures of the Marathi Dalit literary movement, he is known for his debut poetry collection Gavkusabaheril Kavita (1973), his influential theoretical text Dalit Sahitya Swarup Va Bhumika (Dalit Literature: Nature and Role), and his role as a signatory of the political declaration that preceded the formation of the Dalit Panthers. His poems "Mother," "Caste," and "Words" are prescribed texts in university curricula across India and have been translated into English and Swedish. Journalist Praveen Bardapurkar described him as a "One Man Army" — a writer who simultaneously functioned as poet, critic, publisher, editor, and institution-builder.

## Early life and education

Nimbalkar was born on 13 March 1943 in Lanjud (लांजूड), a village in Buldhana district, Maharashtra, into the Mahar community, a Scheduled Caste historically associated with the Ambedkarite Buddhist movement. The title of his debut collection — poems from beyond the village boundary — was drawn directly from the lived geography of caste: the gavkus, or village limit, beyond which Dalit households were historically required to reside.

He pursued higher education at Milind College, Aurangabad, one of the institutions founded by B. R. Ambedkar, where he earned M.A. degrees in History, Hindi, and Dr. Ambedkar Ideology. He later began doctoral research on "Ambedkar's Vision as Reflected in Marathi Poetry" at Rashtrasant Tukadoji Maharaj Nagpur University, but was unable to complete the thesis before his death.

## Literary career

### Founding of Asmitadarsh

In 1967, while based in Aurangabad, Nimbalkar co-founded the Milind Sahitya Sabha alongside R.G. Jadhav, Prof. Raimane, Gangadhar Pantawane, and Sukhram Hivarale. The group launched the journal Asmita — renamed Asmitadarsh after a government objection to the original name — which became the most important periodical of the Marathi Dalit literary movement and continues publication today.

### Poetry

Nimbalkar published three poetry collections over his career:

* Gavkusabaheril Kavita (गावकुसाबाहेरच्या कविता; Poems from Beyond the Village Boundary, 1973, Sugava Prakashan, Pune) — his debut, which established his distinctive voice combining raw realism with Ambedkarite political consciousness. A third edition appeared in 1990.
* Dharmayuddha (धर्मयुद्ध; Holy War) — his second collection, for which he received the Maharashtra Government Literary Award in 1988.
* Vahatya Jakhmanchya Pradesh (वाहत्या जखमांचा प्रदेश; The Land of Flowing Wounds) — his final collection.

His most widely taught poems include:

* Mother (माय) — a poem about a Dalit woman's death from snakebite while gathering firewood; translated by Priya Adarkar and included in Poisoned Bread (1992).
* Caste (जात) — translated by Graham Smith and Jayant Karve; anthologized in An Anthology of Dalit Literature (1992).
* Words (शब्द) — also included in the Anand/Zelliot anthology.

### Critical and theoretical writing

Nimbalkar's most influential prose work is Dalit Sahitya Swarup Va Bhumika (दलित साहित्य स्वरूप व भूमिका; Dalit Literature: Nature and Role), translated into English by Vandana Pathak and P.D. Nimsarkar and published by Prabodhan Prakashan in 2006. The text offers one of the most frequently cited definitions in the field: that Dalit literature encompasses the literature of "Adivasis, vagrant and nomadic tribes, backward class people, workers, laborers, all exploited and afflicted, stricken people." This expansive formulation, broader than strictly caste-based definitions, has been extensively debated in Dalit literary studies.

He also wrote:

* Ambedkari Vicharanchi Disha (The Direction of Ambedkarite Thought)
* Samajik Lokshahiche Pranete Dr. Ambedkar (Dr. Ambedkar: Pioneer of Social Democracy)
* Samajik Krantichi Disha (Direction of Social Revolution) — a compilation of works by Prof. M. Bhi. Chitnis

### Publishing

Nimbalkar founded Prabodhan Prakashan (प्रबोधन प्रकाशन), a publishing house based in Nagpur, through which he produced the weekly newspaper Ajche Prabodhan and the periodical Paricharik. He also contributed to and supported the publications Bhimsandesh, Asmitadarsh, Sumchit, Prabodhan, Lokayat, and Parichariaka. Through Prabodhan Prakashan, he published Lokkavi Wamandada Kardak's Vatchal and poet Yashwant Manohar 's Utthangumfa.

He wrote the column Chalvaliche Divas (Days of the Movement) for the Nagpur edition of Loksatta, an unfinished chronicle of the Ambedkarite movement. A commemorative publication under this title was released in Nagpur on 3 December 2017, his seventh death anniversary.

## Dalit Panthers

Nimbalkar was among the group of Marathi Dalit writers who gathered in the early 1970s at Dadar, Mumbai, forming an intellectual circle that included Namdeo Dhasal, Daya Pawar, Arjun Dangle, J. V. Pawar, and Prahlad Chendwankar.

His most documented political act during this period was signing the public declaration to the press regarding the Elayaperumal Committee report on Dalit atrocities — a statement signed by nine writers including Raja Dhale, Namdeo Dhasal, Arjun Dangle, Bhimrao Shirvale, Umakant Randhir, Gangadhar Pantawane, Waman Nimbalkar, Moreshwar Vahane, and J.V. Pawar. The statement warned that "if the government is unable to prevent injustice and atrocities towards Dalits, we will take the law into our own hands." This declaration was a direct political precursor to the formal founding of the Dalit Panthers on 29 May 1972.

Nimbalkar's role in the Panthers was primarily literary and intellectual. His geographic base in Marathwada and later Vidarbha kept him at a remove from the internal power struggles between factions in Mumbai. He continued to be involved in Ambedkarite movement organizing through his writing, publishing, and public speaking.

## Academic employment

For thirteen years prior to his death, Nimbalkar served as a part-time professor in the Dr. Ambedkar Ideology Department at Rashtrasant Tukadoji Maharaj Nagpur University, Nagpur. He also served as an advisor to the Maharashtra Government Trade Nurses Association from 1978.

## Translations

Nimbalkar's poetry has been translated into English and Swedish.

### English

* An Anthology of Dalit Literature: Poems (ed. Mulk Raj Anand and Eleanor Zelliot, Gyan Publishing House, 1992) — "Mother," "Caste," and "Words," translated by Jayant Karve and Eleanor Zelliot.
* Poisoned Bread: Translations from Modern Marathi Dalit Literature (ed. Arjun Dangle, Orient Longman, 1992; revised ed. Orient BlackSwan, 2009) — "Mother," translated by Priya Adarkar.
* No Entry for the New Sun: Translations from Modern Marathi Dalit Poetry (ed. Arjun Dangle, Disha Books, 1992).

His critical work Dalit Literature: Nature and Role was translated from Marathi by Vandana Pathak and P.D. Nimsarkar (Prabodhan Prakashan, 2006).

### Swedish

Nimbalkar's poetry appears in Detta land som aldrig var vår moder: Dikter av indiska dalitpoeter (This Land That Was Never Our Mother: Poems by Indian Dalit Poets), edited by Vimal Thorat and Eva-Maria Hardtmann (2006), with translations by Jayant Karve.

## Recognition and institutional roles

* Maharashtra Government Literary Award (1988) for Dharmayuddha
* President, 9th All India Dalit Literary Conference (Akhil Bhartiya Dalit Sahitya Sammelan), Nagpur
* President, 10th All India Dalit Literary Conference, Chandrapur
* President, Vidarbha Sahitya Sammelan (organized by Vidarbha Sahitya Sangh)
* President, All India Dalit Literary Corporation (Akhil Bhartiya Dalit Sahitya Mahamandal)
* Listed in the international Who's Who

## Legacy

Three doctoral dissertations and nine M.Phil theses have been written on Nimbalkar's literary works at Indian universities. His poetry is prescribed in M.A. curricula at Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University (Aurangabad), Amravati University, Osmania University (Hyderabad), Savitribai Phule Pune University, Shivaji University (Kolhapur), and Rashtrasant Tukadoji Maharaj Nagpur University. His poems were also included in school curricula in Maharashtra, Gujarat, Karnataka, and Madhya Pradesh.

His poem "Mother" is a prescribed text in the M.A. English programme at Maulana Azad National Urdu University, Hyderabad, and appears in Dalit literature syllabi at Symbiosis College, Pune.

Nimbalkar's publishing house Prabodhan Prakashan, Nagpur, continued to be managed by his widow Snehalata Waman Nimbalkar after his death.

## Death

Nimbalkar died of a heart attack on 3 December 2010 in Nagpur, at the age of 67.
