Object capture is a workflow for reconstructing three-dimensional (3D) digital models from multiple overlapping photographs of a physical object. Typical pipelines combine camera pose estimation (often structure-from-motion), dense reconstruction, mesh generation, and texture mapping.

Object-capture methods are used in cultural-heritage documentation, visual-effects production, product digitization, and downstream 3D-print workflows.

## Workflow

A common object-capture workflow includes:

* Capture of overlapping images from multiple viewpoints.
* Camera pose estimation and sparse scene reconstruction.
* Dense reconstruction into depth maps or point clouds.
* Surface reconstruction into a polygon mesh.
* Texture generation and optional post-processing (for example, hole filling or mesh simplification).

## Applications

### Cultural heritage

Object capture is used to document artifacts, sculptures, and archaeological objects for preservation, measurement, and digital access.

### Media and interactive content

Film and game pipelines use image-based reconstruction to create realistic environment and prop assets from physical scenes and objects.

### 3D printing

Captured meshes are often exported to manufacturing formats (such as OBJ or STL), repaired where needed, and prepared in slicer software before additive manufacturing.

## Limitations

Accuracy can degrade on reflective or transparent surfaces, low-texture objects, or image sets with insufficient overlap and inconsistent lighting.
