Priyam Archi

Priyam Archi is a Bangladeshi actress from internationally screened and award-winning films like No Ground Beneath The Feet, Boli – The Wrestler and Nirvana. Chattogram-originated Priyam played Rashuni in Boli – The Wrestler, a film made in the raw Chatgaiya dialect, won Busan International Film Festival’s top prize New Currents and earned a place in the archives of the Busan Cinema Centre. Priyam attended MIFF with her film Nirvana, which won the Special Jury Award in 2024. The black-and-white silent film was initially planned with one character (Priyam Archi), and was later developed into the inner struggles of three main characters. Both of her films, No Ground Beneath The Feet and Boli – The Wrestler, were Bangladesh’s entries for the Academy Awards (Oscar) in the International Feature Film category in consecutive years (2023 and 2024). As Mina in No Ground Beneath The Feet, she received positive mentions by reviewers; one review noted that Priyam Archi “is equally good in a very difficult role… [and] presents [it] in a highly nuanced fashion”. She was also nominated for the Malaysia Golden Global Awards as Best Supporting Actress for No Ground Beneath The Feet in 2022.

Priyam Archi appeared in Tiktoker Tale: Deshlai, a film by Indian filmmaker Q, which premiered at the Tasveer International Film Festival in Seattle, United States, as the closing film; Priyam represented the film on behalf of the Deshlai team. The Unexpected, produced by Eastern Thespians Wimbledon, a UK-based production company, cast Priyam Archi and had its world premiere at the 16th Chicago South Asian Film Festival.

Kamalapuran, a government-grant-funded short film, cast Priyam Archi and satirised discrimination against women in society. It premiered at the auditorium of the National Art Gallery of Bangladesh Shilpakala Academy in 2017 and is one of her early works. Later, in Icchedana, a three-season series jointly created by UNICEF and the Ministry of Women and Children's Affairs, Priyam Archi played the lead character Tanzila. The series follows an all-girl football team and their community as they strive to avoid child marriage and pursue their ambitions.

Her stage performances include an adaptation of English dramatist J. B. Priestley’s play An Inspector Calls, directed by Naila Azad, in which Priyam Sarker played the girl in the opening suicide scene. She also worked in novelist Shaheen Akhtar’s Shokhi Rongomala, brought to the stage by Bottala Production in 2023.

She completed her MBA from the University of Dhaka.
