Alice Norsworthy is an American marketing executive who serves as President, Global Marketing for Universal Destinations & Experiences, the theme park division of NBCUniversal, a subsidiary of Comcast. She oversees Strategic Planning and Consumer Insights, Integrated Marketing and Experience Design, Corporate Partnerships and Alliances, and Sales and Distribution across Universal's worldwide portfolio of theme park destinations.

Over a career spanning more than three decades in destination entertainment, Norsworthy has held senior marketing leadership roles at Walt Disney World, Royal Caribbean International, and Universal Parks & Resorts. She is widely recognized for leading the marketing strategy behind the launch of The Wizarding World of Harry Potter in 2010 and, most recently, the campaign introducing Epic Universe ahead of its 2025 opening.

## Early life and education

Alice Norsworthy graduated from the University of Florida in Gainesville, Florida in 1981 with a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree. She began her professional career intending to work as an art director, but shifted direction after taking a position at the front desk of the Contemporary Hotel at Walt Disney World while pursuing freelance design work.

## Career

### Walt Disney World (c. 1981–2005)

Norsworthy spent more than fifteen years at Walt Disney World in a sequence of roles spanning marketing, sales, and operations. She progressed through positions of increasing responsibility before moving into an operations leadership role, where she led the development of new products and services. That cross-functional experience—moving from marketing into operations and back—became a defining characteristic of her leadership approach.

### Royal Caribbean International (c. 2005–2008)

In 2005 Norsworthy left Walt Disney World to become Senior Vice President of Marketing at Royal Caribbean International in Miami, Florida. She led the development and implementation of marketing programs that grew the Royal Caribbean brand during that period, including the launch of the Freedom Class of cruise ships—the first to introduce the FlowRider surf simulator. During the mid-2000s, when the broader cruise industry was experiencing a significant downturn in bookings, Norsworthy guided Royal Caribbean to a reported 30 percent increase in sales.

### Universal Parks & Resorts

#### Executive Vice President and Chief Marketing Officer, Universal Orlando (2008–2017)

Norsworthy joined Universal Orlando Resort in September 2008 as Executive Vice President of Marketing and Sales, and subsequently served as Chief Marketing Officer. In that role she led all brand, retail marketing, trade and distribution programs, consumer communication, consumer insights, and corporate partnerships for Universal Orlando, and also guided international brand management efforts for Universal's international theme park destinations.

Her tenure at Universal Orlando included the marketing strategy behind several major attraction launches:

* The Wizarding World of Harry Potter – Hogsmeade (2010): Norsworthy led the cross-functional launch campaign for what became one of the most commercially successful theme park openings in history. The land, developed in close partnership with Warner Bros. and J. K. Rowling 's representatives, opened in June 2010 and quickly drew global attention, driving significant attendance growth at Universal's Islands of Adventure.
* Transformers: The Ride – 3D (2011) and Despicable Me Minion Mayhem (2012): Norsworthy oversaw the branding and launch campaigns for both attractions.
* The Wizarding World of Harry Potter – Diagon Alley (2014): She led marketing for the expansion of the Wizarding World franchise into Universal Studios Florida, framing the two-park experience as "one complete, magical journey."

#### Chief Marketing Officer, Universal Parks & Resorts (2017–2021)

In September 2017 Norsworthy's role expanded to Chief Marketing Officer for the full Universal Parks & Resorts portfolio, encompassing Universal's theme park properties in Orlando, Hollywood, Japan, and Beijing.

#### President, Global Marketing, Universal Destinations & Experiences (2022–present)

Effective January 2022, Norsworthy was appointed President, Global Marketing for Universal Destinations & Experiences—the rebranded parent organization for Universal's global theme park operations. In this role she oversees the Marketing and Sales division in its entirety, with responsibility for ensuring that brand equity and guest demand continue to grow across Universal's worldwide portfolio.

Her most prominent initiative in this role has been the multi-year marketing campaign for Epic Universe, Universal's fifth theme park at its Orlando resort and the first major new U.S. theme park to open in two decades. Planning for the campaign began during the park's development phase; it escalated into a large-scale launch effort that included a global introduction at the 2024 Summer Olympics, an appearance at New York Comic Con in fall 2024, and a Super Bowl LIX commercial in February 2025 that reached an estimated 127 million viewers. A subsequent campaign, "This Changes Everything," introduced during Super Bowl LX in February 2026, extended the brand positioning to encompass Universal Orlando's full four-park resort offering.

## Industry leadership and civic roles

### Brand USA

Norsworthy served on the Board of Directors of Brand USA, the destination marketing organization for the United States, and was elected Chair of the Board in December 2021—a position she held through 2022. Brand USA's board is appointed by the U.S. Secretary of Commerce in consultation with the Secretaries of State and Homeland Security under the Travel Promotion Act.

### Other affiliations

Norsworthy has served on the Board of Directors of the Universal Orlando Foundation, the National Board of Directors of Big Brothers Big Sisters of America, and the Advisory Board of the Eric Friedheim Tourism Institute at the University of Florida. She has also served as an adviser to the United States Travel and Tourism Advisory Board.
