Kabochi Gitau (born November 27, 1993), known by his stage name KABEAUSHÉ, is a Kenyan singer, songwriter, performer, and music producer living in Berlin, whose music ranges from contemporary pop to experimental music. He first gained recognition through releases on the Ugandan label Hakuna Kulala and later on Berlin-based Monkeytown Records.

## Early life and career beginnings

Kabochi Gitau was born and raised in Nairobi, Kenya, in a Christian home. His formative musical influences include gospel, 1970s and 1980s soul music, ragga, and early 2000s pop music.

In addition to his work as a musician, Gitau worked as a radio presenter, actor and in television productions such as MTV Shuga and was also – under the mentorship of Lupita Nyong’o – a participant in MTV Rising Star, which sparked his interest in energetic, dynamic stage shows.

## Music albums

### The Coming of Gaze (2023)

Kabeaushé's compilation album The Coming of Gaze brought together selected tracks from his weekly online series Absorbed Wednesdays, which he regularly uploaded to SoundCloud, and was released in 2023 via the Ugandan label Hakuna Kulala. This mix of pop-oriented songwriting and electronic compositions was curated by Derek Debru and Arlen Dilsizian of Nyege Nyege.

### Hold On to Deer Life, There’s a Black Boy Behind You! (2023)

His debut album, Hold On to Deer Life, There's a Black Boy Behind You!, was released in November 2023 on Monkeytown Records and blends influences from club music, rave, electronic, and experimental music. The album was reviewed in publications including Bandcamp Daily, Loud and Quiet, Pan African Music, and DJ Mag.

The two singles "Go With Gut" and especially "These Dishes Ain't Gonna Do Themselves" gained attention after the album's release. The latter was featured on the official soundtrack of the video game EA Sports FC 24 and in the soundtrack of the British television series Queenie as well as How to Sell Drugs Online Fast.

### Kabeaushé Presents: Iggy Swaggering Ungrateful Incessant Little Peeeaaaaaaa (2025)

At the end of 2024, Kabeaushé announced his second album, Kabeaushé Presents: Iggy Swaggering Ungrateful Incessant Little Peeeaaaaaaa, scheduled for release on February 27th of 2026, and released the three singles "I Don’t Need You, So You Could Tell Me If I’m Gud", "We Have Ourselves A Shekdown" and "Life's Waaaaaaay Too Fleeeeeteen'". The album's concept and visual style are inspired by German and American films of the 1920s, as well as Sergei Eisenstein 's Ivan the Terrible.

## Live performances

In 2023, he played at the Trans Musicales Festival in Rennes, France, and in the following months and years, he performed at more than 45 other festivals across Europe. In 2025, he played the Rock en Seine Festival in Paris, and in 2025, he announced his first headlining tour dates through the German booking agency KKT.

## Music licensing

Kabeaushé’s music has been licensed for film, television, and video game productions. Placements include:

* " These Dishes Ain't Gonna Do Themselves" — featured on the official soundtrack of the UK television series Queenie (2024).
* " These Dishes Ain't Gonna Do Themselves" — included on the EA SPORTS FC 24 official soundtrack and Spotify/EA playlists; also promoted on EA Sports' Instagram.
* " These Dishes Ain't Gonna Do Themselves" — placement in the NBC series Grosse Pointe Garden Society (soundtrack / episode credits).
* " These Dishes Ain't Gonna Do Themselves" — placement in the Netflix series How to Sell Drugs Online (Fast) (music credits / episode soundtrack).

## Awards and nominations

In 2024, Kabeaushé received the Polyton Award for Best Composition and the VUT Indie Award for Best Album.
