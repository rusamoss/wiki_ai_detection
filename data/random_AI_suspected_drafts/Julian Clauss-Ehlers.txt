Julian Clauss-Ehlers is a British-American chef and restaurateur who has worked in New York City since 2000. He is known for his roles as executive chef at Trattoria Bianca and Butcher and Banker at the New Yorker Hotel in Manhattan. In 2022, he co-authored Eating Together, Being Together with psychologist Caroline Clauss-Ehlers, published by Princeton Architectural Press. Since 2025, he has served as Director of Culinary Arts and Hospitality Management at Long Island University Brooklyn.

## Career

### Early career in Europe (1985–1999)

Clauss-Ehlers trained and worked in professional kitchens in France and England during the late 1980s and 1990s, holding positions at several establishments recognised by the Michelin Guide, including Les Prés d'Eugénie in Eugénie-les-Bains, operated by chef Michel Guérard, and La Côte St. Jacques in Joigny. He subsequently moved to England, where he served as Head Chef at Lower Slaughter Manor in Gloucestershire from 1993 to 1995, a property listed in The Good Food Guide and the AA Best Restaurants in Britain guide during that period.

From 1995 to 1999, Clauss-Ehlers owned and operated The Green Dragon, a pub in Buckinghamshire. The establishment was listed in the Michelin Guide to Great Britain and Ireland in 1999 and 2000, and in The Good Pub Guide in 1998 and 1999. The Good Pub Guide named it Buckinghamshire Dining Pub of the Year in 1998. The pub was also covered in The Observer Style Magazine in December 1997.

### New York City (2000–2007)

Clauss-Ehlers relocated to New York City in 2000. In January 2001, he co-founded Zitoune, a French-Moroccan restaurant in the Meatpacking District, where he served as executive chef and co-owner until April 2002. The restaurant received a one-star review from William Grimes in The New York Times, a two-star review from Steve Cuozzo in the New York Post, a profile in The New York Times Magazine by Jonathan Reynolds, and a listing in New York magazine's "Where to Eat Now 2002."

From 2002 to 2004, he served as executive chef consultant at Link Restaurant, Bar and Lounge and 3 Square Café near Union Square. Link was reviewed in Crain's New York Business and mentioned in The New York Times and the Daily News.

From 2004 to 2007, Clauss-Ehlers was executive chef at the Monkey Bar in Midtown Manhattan, operated by the Glazier Group. His appointment was noted in the New York Post, and the restaurant was reviewed in The New York Sun.

During this period, he also presented at the James Beard House First Friday Luncheon (2003–2004), documentation of which is held in the James Beard Foundation Archive at New York University Library.

### New Yorker Hotel (2007–2025)

In 2007, Clauss-Ehlers joined the New Yorker Hotel in Midtown Manhattan as executive chef, overseeing its food and beverage operations. In 2014, he became executive chef of Trattoria Bianca, the hotel's Italian restaurant, a position he held until 2025. Trattoria Bianca was reviewed by food critic Gael Greene in Insatiable Critic.

From 2021 to 2025, he also served as executive chef of Butcher and Banker, a steakhouse and bar at the New Yorker Hotel. Butcher and Banker received an OpenTable Diners' Choice Award in 2024.

### Long Island University Brooklyn (2025–present)

In September 2025, Clauss-Ehlers was appointed Director of Culinary Arts and Hospitality Management at Long Island University Brooklyn, where he has contributed to the development of a Bachelor of Science program in Culinary Arts.

## Eating Together, Being Together (2022)

In 2022, Clauss-Ehlers co-authored Eating Together, Being Together: Recipes, Activities, and Advice from a Chef Dad and Psychologist Mom with Caroline Clauss-Ehlers, published by Princeton Architectural Press. The book addresses cooking and family wellness through recipes and activities. Following its release, the authors appeared on NPR 's WNYC All Of It with Allison Stewart and on WPIX NY.

## Television

Clauss-Ehlers appeared in Season 2, Episode 13 of Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives on Food Network in 2008 and was included in the companion book by Guy Fieri published by HarperCollins.

## Personal life

Julian Clauss-Ehlers holds dual citizenship in the United Kingdom and the United States. He is married to Caroline Clauss-Ehlers, a professor of psychology at Long Island University Brooklyn. He is fluent in French.
