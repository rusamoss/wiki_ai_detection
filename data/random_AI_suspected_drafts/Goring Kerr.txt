## History

Goring Kerr was established in 1947 in Windsor, Berkshire, by Bruce Goring Kerr and David Hiscock. The company pioneered the use of electromagnetic induction for industrial contaminant detection. In 1948, the company achieved a significant milestone when Mars, Incorporated became its first major customer, utilizing Goring Kerr's "Metlokate" system to inspect chocolate bars at its UK production facilities.

In the 1980s, Goring Kerr PLC was a publicly traded company on the London Stock Exchange and held a dominant position in the global food and pharmaceutical inspection sectors.

The company underwent several high-profile corporate transitions:

1988: Acquired by Graseby PLC for £20.8 million, operating thereafter as Graseby Goring Kerr.

1997: Graseby PLC was acquired by Smiths Industries.

1998: Smiths Industries sold the Goring Kerr business unit to Thermo Electron (now Thermo Fisher Scientific) for $43 million.

2011: Thermo Fisher Scientific sold the legacy Goring Kerr metal detection and X-ray product lines to Eriez Magnetics.
