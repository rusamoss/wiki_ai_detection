Amjad Raza Jauhari is a Pakistani Shia Islamic scholar, religious orator, and public speaker known for delivering majalis in Pakistan and abroad. He belongs to the Jauhari family of religious scholars. He is the youngest son of Pakistani Shia Cleric Talib Jauhari.

## Early life and education

Amjad Raza Jauhari was born in Karachi, Pakistan. He belongs to the Jauhari family known for its contributions to Shia religious scholarship. His father, Talib Jauhari, was a well-known Shia scholar, poet, and religious leader.

Jauhari pursued religious studies at the Hawza Ilmiyya in Qom, Iran, one of the major centers of Shia Islamic learning.

## Religious career

Amjad Raza Jauhari delivers majalis (religious sermons) related to Shia Islam, particularly during the months of Muharram and Safar. His lectures focus on Islamic history, the events of Karbala, and the teachings of the Ahl al-Bayt.

He participates in religious gatherings across Pakistan and within Shia communities abroad. His Sham-e-Ghareeban majalis during Muharram have been broadcast on Pakistani television channels.

## Public presence and media coverage

Amjad Raza Jauhari has participated in major religious gatherings and public commemorations in Pakistan, particularly during Muharram and Youm-e-Ali observances.

In March 2026, he was reported among scholars participating in Youm-e-Ali processions in Karachi. Independent media outlets including BOL News and The Express Tribune covered the event and mentioned his participation in religious proceedings connected to the observance.

## Family

Amjad Raza Jauhari belongs to a family of religious scholars. His grandfather, Maulana Mustafa Jauhar, was also a religious scholar, while his father Talib Jauhari was a prominent Pakistani Shia cleric, poet, and former member of the Sindh Provincial Assembly.
