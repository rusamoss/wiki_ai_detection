Nesara Bay City (stylized as NESARA Bay City) is a proposed planned community development on the southern coastline of Lombok, Indonesia. Formerly known as Marina Bay City, the project was rebranded in early 2026 after being acquired by Lux Property, a company associated with Australian developer Jamie McIntyre. The development is notable for its proposed prohibition on interest-bearing loans for property purchases within the city.

## History

The site was originally marketed as Marina Bay City, a proposed high-density development featuring towers up to 30 storeys in a style described as " Dubai -inspired." In January 2026, Australian media outlet Australian National Review announced that the project had been acquired by Lux Property and would be completely redesigned under the new name Nesara Bay City.

The project is associated with a broader set of proposed developments in South Lombok attributed to Jamie McIntyre, including Harbourside City in the Blongas area and FuturisCity.com, though these remain unconfirmed and conceptual.

## Design and Concept

Following the rebranding, the development's master plan was significantly altered. The previous high-rise concept was abandoned in favor of a "low-rise, low-density" design intended to integrate with the natural landscape. The revised plan emphasizes "village-style precincts," extensive green space, and architecture intended to reflect Indonesian coastal culture.

## Interest-Free Property Model

A defining feature of Nesara Bay City is its proposed prohibition on interest-bearing loans for property purchases. Under the announced model:

* Residential buyers would secure villas with a 20% deposit and pay the balance progressively during construction without interest
* Investors would provide a 40% deposit, with the remaining balance paid through rental income over three to five years
* Traditional mortgage financing from banks would not be used within the development

This model has been described by the developer as intended to eliminate " mortgage servitude" and create an "interest-free property city."

## Proposed Amenities

According to promotional materials, the planned city would include:

* Eco-friendly architecture using sustainable materials
* Organic farms and local markets
* Wellness centers and holistic health facilities
* Co-working hubs with high-speed internet
* Cultural and educational facilities
* A marina with ocean-view villas

## Development Status

As of early 2026, the project remains in the conceptual stage and has not yet received formal approval from the Lombok provincial government or relevant Indonesian ministries. The developer has indicated that pending approvals, construction timelines have not been established.

Some Indonesian media reports have noted that if approval is delayed, the concept might be relocated to other Indonesian islands more open to foreign investment.
