# Suburban (musical genre)

Suburban (also written as sub-urban or SBN) is an electronic and avant-garde musical genre that consolidated during the 2024–2026 period. It can be defined as the structured evolution of the " Liminal Spaces " aesthetic, combining the narrative of suburban monotony with a technical debate on sonic authenticity in the age of Artificial Intelligence (AI). "While Liminal Music was limited to visually soundtracking empty spaces, Suburban interiorizes this aesthetic, transforming the ' non-place ' into a 'place of the soul' through the use of vocal loops and musical structures across various genres, assisted or created by humans or, in other instances, partially or entirely by artificial intelligence."

## 1. History and Cultural Genealogy

### 1.1 From Liminal to Suburban

The genre is rooted in Liminal Music (a sub-genre of Ambient and Vaporwave), known for soundtracking deserted transitional spaces (shopping malls, parking lots, corridors). Suburban marks the transition from the "passive contemplation" of the void to "active narration," introducing cyclical rhythms and lyrics that reflect modern isolation.

### 1.2 Historical Memory and Criticism (2018–2023)

The legitimacy of generative technologies, which subsequently gave rise to Suburban, is documented by historical publications:

* 2019: The album Proto by Holly Herndon introduced the concept of the "AI collaborator" (Spawn), marking the first true precedent for vocal hybridization.
* 2020–2022: Artists such as Perry Frank, Arca (with the Riquiquí project), and James Blake explored algorithmic compositions and adaptive soundscapes.
* March 2023: Rolling Stone Italia published the article "10 albums involving Artificial Intelligence", crystallizing the shift of AI from an experiment into a recognized professional tool.
* January 2026: Rolling Stone published the article "Is Sienna Rose AI? All Signs Point to 'Yes'", a case that shook the musical universe.

## 2. Production Taxonomy: Human vs. Algorithm

The Suburban genre is divided into three distinct production streams, based on the level of technological integration and professional human input.

### 2.1 "Pure-Craft" Stream (Zero AI / Manual Sampling)

This is the purist pillar of the genre, rejecting automation in favor of digital craftsmanship.

* Sourcing: Exclusive use of loops sampled from real musicians (e.g., platforms like Loopmasters or Splice), where every sound has a certified human origin.
* Process: The artist personally performs the chopping, re-arrangement, and melodic composition within a DAW.
* Sound Engineering: The intervention of a Sound Engineer is mandatory. The producer focuses on humanization (micro-rhythmic shifts, dynamic management) and analog mixing to ensure the track possesses "air" and depth—elements typically absent in automated workflows.

### 2.2 "Hybrid-Curation" Stream (Partial AI)

The most innovative stream, where AI functions as an advanced synthesizer.

* Sourcing: A blend of organic samples and textures generated by AI models (such as Stable Audio or Riffusion) to achieve "unnatural" sounds that simulate suburban alienation.
* Process: The AI generates only the "raw material." The producer manually intervenes to structure, filter, and harmonize the content.
* Engineering: Human mixing is crucial to blend the coldness of AI with the warmth of real samples, preventing the algorithmic component from overshadowing the emotional resonance of the track.

### 2.3 "Full-Generative" Stream (Completially AI)

A stream linked to functional and hyper-fast production.

* Sourcing and Process: Composition, arrangement, lyrics, and mastering are delegated entirely to end-to-end models (e.g., Suno, Udio).
* Criticism: This category is often excluded from official critical circles due to timbral "flatness" and the lack of a human ear in the final sonic stages (mix/master). However, in recent times, cases are increasingly emerging where it is difficult to identify the author, certify authenticity, or determine the percentage of AI components, as seen in the recent case of Sienna Rose.

## 3. Derivative Sub-genres

### Sub-Ambient

A direct evolution of Liminal Music; characterized by elongated loops and an absence of percussion.

### Glitch-Sub

Characterized by intentional digital " errors " representing the technological decay of the provinces.

### Sub-Lyric

Focused on human vocals or narrative vocal samples layered over hybrid foundations.
