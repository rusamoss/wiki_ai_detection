Joe Young is an American filmmaker, cartoonist, writer, producer, and nonprofit executive. He is the creator of Kemet the Time Traveler, a national comic and educational franchise distributed through the National Newspaper Publishers Association (NNPA). Young is the founder and CEO of Joe Young Entertainment, LLC, and the creator and executive producer of the televised talent competition Hartford’s Got Talent. His productions have been covered by the Associated Press, Google News, NNPA outlets, and regional NBC, ABC, and FOX affiliates.

Young is also the founder of CAN DO, Inc., a Hartford-based youth media arts nonprofit. In recognition of his work, the State of Connecticut proclaimed “Joe Young Day” in 2024, and the City of Hartford proclaimed “Joe Young Day” in 1991.

## Early life and education

Young was born in Hartford, Connecticut. He began drawing at an early age and developed an interest in cartooning and animation throughout his youth. He earned a Master’s degree in Organizational Leadership from Goodwin University.

## Career

### Early career: Scruples and HAFI

Young gained recognition as the creator of Scruples, a comic strip launched in the late 1980s and syndicated regionally through the 1990s. He later founded the Hartford Animation & Film Institute (HAFI), where he produced animation programming and workshops.

In 1999, Young created the World’s Longest Comic Strip, an 8-foot-tall, 100-yard-long installation using Scruples characters. That same year, he received the Daily Point of Light Award from the White House.

### Joe Young Entertainment, LLC

Young founded Joe Young Entertainment, LLC, a production company specializing in comics, animation, television, and educational media. The company produces the Kemet franchise, Hartford’s Got Talent, and youth-centered digital media projects.

### Kemet the Time Traveler

Young created Kemet the Time Traveler, an educational comic and media property exploring African American history and world cultures. The franchise includes nine comic books, national activity sheets published in over 230 NNPA newspapers, a live mascot, and an animated adaptation in development.

### Film and television

#### Kemet: One Nation, Many Journeys (2025)

Young wrote and produced the educational PSA Kemet: One Nation, Many Journeys, filmed at the Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island with National Park Service approval. It was distributed nationally through the AP news wire and Google News, and aired on FOX61 and other affiliates. The PSA received a Platinum AVA Digital Award.

A 22-minute educational version is currently in production.

#### Hartford’s Got Talent

Young is the creator and executive producer of Hartford’s Got Talent, a regional televised talent competition.

#### Kemet & Me

Young produces Kemet & Me, a children’s educational series featuring the Kemet mascot.

### CAN DO, Inc.

Young founded CAN DO, Inc., a nonprofit offering training in filmmaking, comic arts, digital media, and entrepreneurship.

### Board and community roles

Young’s leadership roles include:

* Vice Chair, Board of Trustees, University of Bridgeport
* Board Member, Connecticut Film & TV Alliance
* Member, Education Committee, The Bushnell Center for the Performing Arts
* Member, BZ Fraternity, supporting scholarships for students of color
