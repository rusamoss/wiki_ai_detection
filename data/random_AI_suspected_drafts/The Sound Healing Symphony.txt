## The Sound Healing Symphony

The Sound Healing Symphony is a large-scale sound bath concert series founded in San Francisco, California, by Guy Douglas and Simona Marie Asinovski of Sound Meditation Presents, first emerging around 2016–2017 at Grace Cathedral. The event has been described as attracting crowds of over 1,000–1,400 attendees per session and is referred to as the largest sound bath in the United States.

## History

The series began in 2016–17 in San Francisco's Grace Cathedral and quickly grew in popularity. It has since expanded to other West Coast cities and venues, such as the Portland Art Museum and locations across Los Angeles and New York.

## Format and Concept

Featuring a rotating collective of musicians, The Sound Healing Symphony immerses participants in layered soundscapes using instruments such as gongs, crystal singing bowls, Tibetan bowls, Native American flutes, didgeridoo, Celtic harp, and shamanic drums.

## Notable Musicians

The Sound Healing Symphony has featured a rotating collective of musicians, including several performers who participated in its NBC Today Show appearance:

* Guy Douglas, co-founder and multi-instrumentalist, known for his gong and crystal singing bowl performances
* Sarah Glass, singer featured in the Today Show segment
* Russell Feingold, flutist & didgeridoo player, appearing in major performances and the Today Show
* Dan Neville, vibraphone player, also part of the NBC Today performance
* Destiny Muhammad, noted jazz harpist, featured in select performances
* Simona Marie Asinovski, co-founder and musician, involved in organizing and guiding the ensemble's vision

Additional musicians who have been involved since the inception of the series include:

* Alisa Rose
* Vladimir Carmeda
* Loriel Starr
* Xenia Giolli

While independent press coverage is limited for these founding members, they have been integral to the development and continuity of the ensemble.

## Reception

Media have widely covered the event:

* In 2018, Business Insider reported that "1,300 people… sprawl across the floor of Grace Cathedral" for a 90-minute session with "gongs, crystal bowls, a harp, and a didgeridoo".
* The ensemble was also featured on NBC's Today Show, performing live on air with Guy Douglas on gongs and singing bowls.
* In April 2017, KPIX CBS News Bay Area featured the sound bath events, with co-founder Simona Asinovski explaining: "What we try to do is create an environment for people to come in and really unplug from the day, unplug from their life for an hour." The report noted that events at both the Conservatory of Flowers and Grace Cathedral were sold out.
* SF Yoga Magazine celebrated its communal scale and immersive design.
* SFGATE's 2017 report emphasized the large lines and sold-out crowds, with co-founders commenting on its national prominence.
* Additionally, SFGATE's cultural column in April 2025 reaffirmed the event's presence in San Francisco's vibrant arts scene, with recent gatherings of over 1,000 people.
