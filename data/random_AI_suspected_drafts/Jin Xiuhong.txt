Jin Xiuhong (also known as Jane Jin) is a Chinese pro-democracy activist based in the United States. She has participated in overseas Chinese democracy movements and public demonstrations related to human rights issues in China.

## Background

Jin Xiuhong has been described in media reports as a participant in the 1989 Tiananmen Square protests. She later became active in pro-democracy advocacy efforts among overseas Chinese communities in the United States.

## Activism

Jin Xiuhong has taken part in public demonstrations and advocacy activities concerning political and human rights issues in China.

During a visit by Chinese President Xi Jinping to Seattle in 2015, she was among protesters and was quoted in international media coverage of the demonstrations.

She has also been reported to participate in protests and advocacy events organized by overseas Chinese pro-democracy groups.

Coverage by Voice of America has documented her involvement in commemorative activities related to the Tiananmen Square protests and in demonstrations by Chinese pro-democracy organizations abroad.

Local media reports have also described her participation in public memorial events in Seattle.

## Organizational involvement

Jin Xiuhong has been associated with overseas Chinese pro-democracy networks in the United States, including groups connected to the Chinese democracy movement.

According to organizational sources, she has been listed among leadership members of organizations linked to the Federation for a Democratic China.
