Lau Woei Jye (Chinese: 刘伟杰) is an academic specializing in membrane technology. He is a full professor at Universiti Teknologi Malaysia, having been promoted to that position at the age of 41.

Lau's research involves membrane-based separation processes, including thin-film composite membranes, nanofiltration, and reverse osmosis systems. These technologies are used in water purification and industrial wastewater treatment. According to Google Scholar data, Lau's published work has been cited over 24,000 times, and his h-index is 80.

Lau serves on the editorial boards of the International Water Association journal Water Reuse, the Institution of Chemical Engineers journal Chemical Engineering Research and Design and the Springer Nature journal ENGINEERING Chemical Engineering. He also serves as the Technical Consultant for IGNIS Environment Initiatives Sdn. Bhd.

He was a recipient of the Department of Education (Australia) ’s Endeavour Research Fellowship in 2015 and Akademi Sains Malaysia 's Top Research Scientists Malaysia (TRSM) in 2023.

Since 2019, Lau has served as a coordinator for the Malaysia Alumni of the Sakura Science Association (MASSA). MASSA is affiliated with the Sakura Science Club, which is led by the Japan Science and Technology Agency.

Lau has published commentary on wastewater recycling, desalination, and research integrity in The Star (Malaysia) and New Straits Times. He has also given interviews to MStar and ChemistryViews (a publication of Chemistry Europe). Separately, he has been involved in university–industry collaborations related to semiconductor manufacturing.

## Research

Lau develops advanced membrane materials for desalination and wastewater reuse. His work has focused on practical challenges including membrane fouling, energy efficiency, and water recovery from industrial sources.

One of his most cited publications is a review titled A Recent Progress in Thin Film Composite Membrane: A Review, which has received nearly 895 citations.

In 2022, one of Lau's research innovations was shortlisted in the KANS (Knowledge Application and Notion for Society) competition, organized by the Mustafa Science and Technology Foundation — the same organization behind the Mustafa Prize.

Lau also contributed to the edited volume 60 Years of the Loeb–Sourirajan Membrane, published by Elsevier.

Lau has delivered invited lectures at universities and scientific conferences in Malaysia and in other regions.
