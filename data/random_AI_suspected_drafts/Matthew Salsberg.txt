Matthew Salsberg (born 1971) is a Canadian-born American television writer and producer and professional poker player. He is known for his work as a senior executive producer on the Showtime series Weeds and for his accomplishments in tournament poker, including winning the World Poker Tour Player of the Year award and multiple major live poker titles. His career has been the subject of profile and feature coverage in poker and entertainment trade publications.

## Early life and education

Salsberg was born in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. He was raised in a conservative Jewish household and attended Jewish day schools and summer camps. His father, Eli Salsberg, worked as a men's clothing contractor, and his mother, Lynn Salsberg, was a nurse. He has two older sisters.

Salsberg graduated from Concordia University in Montreal in 1994 with a degree in urban studies. He relocated to Los Angeles in 1997 to pursue a career in television writing and production.

## Television career

Salsberg began his television career in the late 1990s, writing for the children's mystery series The Mystery Files of Shelby Woo. From 1999 to 2001, he worked as a story editor on the Canadian series Big Wolf on Campus, for which he received a Gemini Award nomination for Best Writing.

In the early 2000s, Salsberg worked as a staff writer on NBC's The Jake Effect and sold multiple story ideas to HBO's Curb Your Enthusiasm, where he later contributed in a consulting and creative capacity.

Salsberg went on to serve as an executive producer on Showtime's Weeds. In industry coverage, Deadline described him as a senior creative figure on the series and a "top lieutenant" of series creator Jenji Kohan.

During and after his tenure on Weeds, Salsberg developed several television projects. In 2009, he was reported to be developing the comedy pilot Me & Lee, according to an industry development update published by The Futon Critic. Deadline later reported that Salsberg and Kohan were writing and executive producing a comedy project titled Whales for Showtime, and that Kohan was executive producing Me & Lee, a project written by Salsberg.

In subsequent years, Salsberg continued to develop and write television projects for various studios and networks, while also working as a consulting producer on Showtime's Kidding.

## Poker career

Alongside his television work, Salsberg built a parallel career as a professional poker player. He achieved major recognition in 2012, winning the World Poker Tour event in Paris and earning the World Poker Tour Player of the Year award that season.

Salsberg recorded a deep run in the 2011 World Series of Poker Main Event, finishing 70th overall.

In 2024, Salsberg won the WSOP Circuit Main Event at Commerce Casino in Los Angeles, earning $286,134 and a WSOP Circuit ring. Coverage of the victory noted his background as a Hollywood television producer in addition to his poker accomplishments.

As of 2024, Salsberg has accumulated approximately $3.9 million in recorded live tournament earnings and ranks among the top Canadian players by number of cashes in major live events.

## Personal life

In 2021, Salsberg was diagnosed with metastatic colon cancer. Following treatment, he returned to professional activities in both television development and poker competition in subsequent years.
