Boarderie is an American direct-to-consumer specialty food company based in West Palm Beach, Florida, that produces and ships pre-arranged charcuterie, cheese, and dessert boards across the United States. Founded in 2020, the company received national media attention after appearing on the television series Shark Tank and after setting a Guinness World Records title for the largest charcuterie board.

## History

Boarderie was founded in 2020 by Aaron Menitoff and Rachel Solomon, who had previously operated a catering business in the Palm Beach area of Florida. After the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted their catering operations, the founders shifted to a mail-order model, initially under the name Cheeseboarder, and launched a direct-to-consumer e-commerce platform offering fully assembled charcuterie boards shipped overnight nationwide.

In 2022, Solomon and Menitoff appeared on the fourteenth season of Shark Tank, where they accepted an investment from Lori Greiner. Following the broadcast, the company reported substantial revenue growth.

On October 4, 2023, Boarderie assembled a charcuterie board weighing approximately 769 pounds (349 kg) and measuring about 20 by 14 feet at the Colony Hotel in Palm Beach, Florida. Guinness World Records verified it the same evening as the largest charcuterie board, surpassing the previous record. The board was a scaled-up replica of the company's "Arte" board and was assembled by a team of about 40 staff.

## Products

Boarderie's boards are composed of cheeses, cured meats, crackers, nuts, dried fruits, chocolates, and spreads, and are sold in several sizes intended to serve varying numbers of people. The company also offers dessert boards and seasonal or holiday-themed boards. Boards are assembled at the company's Florida facility, shipped overnight in insulated packaging, and intended to be refrigerated before serving.

## Reception

Boarderie's boards have been reviewed by several food and lifestyle publications. Taste of Home, PureWow, and Buy Side from WSJ published hands-on reviews describing the boards as high in quality but premium in price, and Wired reviewed the company's dessert board. AOL also tested and reviewed the delivery service. The company's products were named to Oprah's Favorite Things in 2022 and 2023.
