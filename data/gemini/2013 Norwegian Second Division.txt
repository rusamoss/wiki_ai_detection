The **2013 Norwegian Second Division** (referred to as *Oddsen-ligaen* for sponsorship reasons) was the third-tier football league season in Norway. The competition began in April 2013 and concluded in October 2013. 

The league comprised 56 clubs divided into four regional groups of 14 teams each. At the end of the 26-round regular season, the winner of each group earned direct promotion to the 2014 1. divisjon (the second tier of Norwegian football), while the bottom three teams in each group were relegated to the 3. divisjon.

---

## Season Overview

The 2013 campaign was marked by the quick rebound of three clubs that had been relegated from the 1. divisjon at the end of the 2012 season: Bærum SK, Alta IF, and Tromsdalen UIL all won their respective groups to secure immediate returns to the second tier. 

The fourth group winner, Nest-Sotra, achieved a historic milestone by winning Group 3, earning the club its first-ever promotion to the second level of the Norwegian football league system.

---

## League Format

* **Teams:** 56 total (14 per group)
* **Matches:** 26 per team (home and away double round-robin)
* **Points System:** 3 points for a win, 1 point for a draw, 0 points for a loss
* **Promotion:** Group champions (4 teams total) automatically promoted to the 1. divisjon.
* **Relegation:** Teams finishing 12th, 13th, and 14th in each group (12 teams total) automatically relegated to the 3. divisjon.
* **Reserve Teams:** B-teams of Eliteserien clubs participated in the league but were ineligible for promotion to tiers higher than the 1. divisjon, and could not play in the same division as their senior team.

## Group Summaries

### Group 1
Group 1 featured a tight race at the top, but Bærum SK managed to hold off strong challenges from teams like Asker and Førde to finish first. Having been relegated from the 1. divisjon in 2012, Bærum mounted a successful campaign powered by an efficient attack, securing promotion back to the second tier with matches to spare. At the lower end of the table, Nesodden, Østsiden, and Birkebeineren struggled throughout the campaign and were relegated.

### Group 2
Alta IF dominated Group 2, asserting control early in the season and building a comfortable lead over contenders such as Raufoss and Fram Larvik. Alta's strong defensive stability and consistent home record ensured an immediate return to the 1. divisjon following their 2012 relegation. The relegation zone saw Strømsgodset 2, Skarp, and Bossekop fail to preserve their status in the third tier.

### Group 3
Group 3 provided one of the season's notable storylines as Nest-Sotra put together a strong run to capture top spot ahead of established sides like Vindbjart and Notodden. Led by effective offensive play, the Sotra-based club earned its first promotion to the second level in club history. Meanwhile, Fløy, Ålgård, and Arendal fought hard in the middle section of the table, while Egersund, Åsane, and Viking 2 avoided the drop. The three teams relegated from Group 3 were Arna-Bjørnar, Eidsvold Turn, and Vidar.

### Group 4
In Group 4, Tromsdalen UIL maintained a strong, consistent campaign to secure first place ahead of second-placed KFUM Oslo. Tromsdalen demonstrated remarkable tactical discipline throughout the 26 fixtures, ensuring a quick return to the 1. divisjon alongside fellow 2012-relegated clubs Bærum and Alta. At the bottom of the table, Senja, Skedsmo, and Neset were unable to escape the relegation zone and fell to the 3. divisjon.

## Summary of Outcomes

### Promoted to 1. divisjon
* **Group 1:** Bærum SK
* **Group 2:** Alta IF
* **Group 3:** Nest-Sotra
* **Group 4:** Tromsdalen UIL

### Relegated to 3. divisjon
* **Group 1:** Birkebeineren, Østsiden, Nesodden
* **Group 2:** Bossekop, Skarp, Strømsgodset 2
* **Group 3:** Arna-Bjørnar, Eidsvold Turn, Vidar
* **Group 4:** Senja, Skedsmo, Neset
