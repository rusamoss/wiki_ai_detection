**Ahmed Abdisalam Adan** (Somali: *Ahmad Cabdisaalaan Aadan*, Arabic: أحمد عبد السلام أدَن) is a Somali politician, diplomat, and media executive. He served as the Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Information in the Transitional Federal Government (TFG) of Somalia during the late 2000s, and later held diplomatic posts, including Ambassador to Ethiopia and Permanent Representative to the African Union. Prior to his political career, he co-founded HornAfrik Media Inc., the first independent radio station established in Mogadishu after the collapse of the central government in 1991.

---

## Early Life and Education

Adan grew up in Somalia before pursuing higher education and work abroad. During the 1990s, following the outbreak of the Somali Civil War, he resided in Canada as part of the Somali diaspora. He studied in Ottawa, Ontario, earning a degree from Carleton University. During his time in Canada, he was active in community advocacy and public broadcasting, experience that later influenced his efforts to rebuild independent media in post-conflict Somalia.

---

## Media Career

In 1999, Adan returned to Somalia alongside fellow Somali-Canadian entrepreneurs, including Ali Iman Sharmarke and Mohamed Elmi, to establish **HornAfrik Media Inc.** Launched in Mogadishu, HornAfrik was a landmark enterprise, operating as the country's first private, independent radio and television network. 

The station provided neutral news coverage, public phone-in talk shows, and educational programming in an environment previously dominated by factional propaganda and armed warlords. Despite severe security challenges, including extortion, intimidation, and targeted attacks by extremist groups and local militias, HornAfrik grew into one of the most prominent media outlets in the Horn of Africa. Adan served as a senior director and spokesman for the network until his transition into public office.

---

## Political Career

### Transitional Federal Government (TFG)

Adan entered national politics during the mandate of the Transitional Federal Government (TFG). In late 2007, Prime Minister Nur Hassan Hussein ("Nur Adde") appointed Adan to the cabinet as **Deputy Prime Minister** and **Minister of Information, Youth, and Sports**. 

His tenure coincided with intense conflict in Mogadishu between government forces (supported by Ethiopian troops and AMISOM) and insurgent factions, including the Islamic Courts Union and Al-Shabaab. As Minister of Information, Adan advocated for political reconciliation, press freedom, and dialogue with moderate opposition figures.

In August 2008, Adan survived an assassination attempt when a roadside bomb exploded near his convoy in Mogadishu, an attack attributed to anti-government insurgents. Despite sustaining injuries, he returned to public service after receiving medical treatment.

### Diplomatic Service

Following his tenure in the cabinet, Adan transitioned into Somalia's foreign service:

* **Ambassador to Ethiopia**: Appointed as Somalia’s envoy to Addis Ababa, he worked to manage bilateral relations, regional security cooperation, and border management between the two neighboring nations.
* **Representative to the African Union**: In addition to his bilateral post in Ethiopia, he served as Somalia’s Permanent Representative to the African Union (AU) and the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA), playing a key role in coordinating the mandate of the African Union Mission in Somalia (AMISOM).
