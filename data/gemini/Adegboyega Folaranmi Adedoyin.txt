Omoba Adegboyega Folaranmi Adedoyin (11 September 1922 – 31 January 2014) was a Nigerian prince, obstetrician-gynecologist, and track and field athlete. He gained international distinction as the first Nigerian competitor to reach a final at the Olympic Games, representing Great Britain at the 1948 Summer Olympics in London. He was also the first African graduate of Queen's University Belfast, earning his medical degree in 1949.

## Early Life and Royal Lineage

Adegboyega Adedoyin was born on 11 September 1922 in Sagamu, Ogun State, Southern Nigeria. He was a son of Christopher William Adedoyin, the Akarigbo (king) of Remoland. As a descendant of one of the ruling houses of the Remo people, he held the traditional title of Omoba (Prince).

In 1942, during World War II, Adedoyin traveled to Northern Ireland to pursue higher education at Queen's University Belfast (QUB).

## Athletic Career

Adedoyin began participating in track and field events shortly after arriving in Belfast. Standing 180 cm (5 ft 11 in) tall, he excelled in both high jump and long jump, while also competing in sprint hurdles.

### Collegiate and Domestic Achievements

During his time at Queen's University, Adedoyin established himself as one of the premier collegiate jumpers in the United Kingdom:

* 1945: Set a new Queen's University high jump record.
* 1946: Won the Universities Athletics Union (UAU) championship high jump title with a clearance of 6 ft 1 in (1.85 m).
* 1947: Captured the high jump title at the Amateur Athletic Association (AAA) Championships at White City Stadium in London, clearing 1.93 meters (6 ft 4 in).

### 1948 Summer Olympics

Because Nigeria did not yet have an International Olympic Committee (IOC)-recognized National Olympic Committee prior to its independence, Adedoyin represented Great Britain at the 1948 Summer Olympics in London. His inclusion made him the first Nigerian native to compete at the Olympic Games.

At the Games, Adedoyin entered both the high jump and long jump events:

* High Jump: He cleared the qualifying height of 1.87 meters to advance to a 20-man final. In the final on 30 July, he cleared 1.90 meters on his third attempt, placing 12th overall.
* Long Jump: On 31 July, he advanced through the preliminary round and placed 5th in the Olympic final with a leap of 7.27 meters. He finished as the top-ranked European competitor in the event, trailing only three American jumpers and an Australian.

## Medical Career and Return to Nigeria

Adedoyin completed his medical studies at Queen's University Belfast in 1949, specializing in obstetrics and gynecology. His graduation made him the university's first Black African alumnus in medicine.

Following a brief period working in Liverpool and Sierra Leone, he returned to Nigeria to practice medicine as an obstetrician-gynecologist. He spent the remainder of his professional career serving in Nigeria's healthcare sector.

## Personal Life and Legacy

After graduating from medical school in 1949, Prince Adedoyin married Hannah Hotoba-During, a Sierra Leonean who was raised in Belfast. He was the uncle of Falilat Ogunkoya, an Olympic medalist for Nigeria in track and field.

In September 2012, the Ogun State Government formally honored Prince Adedoyin in recognition of his historical status as Nigeria's pioneer Olympian.

Adedoyin died in Abeokuta, Ogun State, Nigeria, on 31 January 2014 at the age of 91.
