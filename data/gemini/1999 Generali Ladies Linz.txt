The **1999 Generali Ladies Linz** was a women's tennis tournament played on indoor carpet courts at the Design Center Linz in Linz, Austria. It was the 13th edition of the tournament and was classified as a Tier II event on the 1999 WTA Tour. The tournament took place from 25 October to 31 October 1999, featuring a total prize fund of $520,000.

Top seed Mary Pierce won the singles title, defeating fellow Frenchwoman Sandrine Testud in the final, while the team of Irina Spîrlea and Caroline Vis secured the doubles title.

## Champions

### Singles

  **Mary Pierce** defeated   Sandrine Testud, 6–0, 6–1

* It was Mary Pierce's second WTA singles title of the 1999 season and the 13th of her career.

### Doubles

  **Irina Spîrlea** /   **Caroline Vis** defeated   Tina Križan /   Katarina Srebotnik, 6–4, 6–3

* It was the 6th doubles title of Spîrlea's career and the 7th for Vis.

## Finals Summary

### Singles Final
In an all-French final, top seed Mary Pierce delivered a dominant performance against fifth seed Sandrine Testud. Pierce lost only one game throughout the match, sealing a resounding 6–0, 6–1 victory in under an hour to claim her first Generali Ladies Linz title.

### Doubles Final
In the doubles competition, the unseeded Romanian-Dutch pairing of Irina Spîrlea and Caroline Vis defeated the Slovenian duo of Tina Križan and Katarina Srebotnik in straight sets (6–4, 6–3) to take the championship.
