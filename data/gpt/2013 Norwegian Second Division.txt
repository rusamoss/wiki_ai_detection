'''2013 Norwegian Second Division'''

The '''2013 Norwegian Second Division''' was the 23rd season of the third tier of [[Football in Norway|Norwegian league football]] under the name ''2. divisjon''. The competition was contested by 56 teams, divided into four groups of 14 clubs each. The season began in April 2013 and ended in October 2013.

The league served as the third level of the [[Norwegian football league system]], below the [[2013 Tippeligaen|Tippeligaen]] and the [[2013 Norwegian First Division|First Division]]. Teams played a double round-robin schedule within their group, facing each opponent twice for a total of 26 matches.

The four group winners were promoted to the [[2014 Norwegian First Division|2014 First Division]], while the lowest-placed teams in each group were relegated to the [[2014 Norwegian Third Division|Third Division]].

== Format ==
The Second Division was organized into four parallel groups, each containing 14 teams. Every club played 26 matches, with 3 points awarded for a win, 1 for a draw, and 0 for a loss.

At the end of the season:
* The winner of each group was promoted to the First Division.
* The bottom three teams in each group were relegated to the Third Division.
* Additional relegation adjustments could occur depending on the number of Norwegian clubs relegated from higher divisions, but the standard format called for 12 teams to go down in total.

== Teams ==
The league consisted of returning teams from the 2012 season, clubs relegated from the First Division, and promoted sides from the Third Division.

== Group summaries ==

=== Group 1 ===
Group 1 largely featured teams from eastern and southeastern Norway. The promotion race was closely contested, while the lower half of the table saw several clubs fighting to avoid relegation until the closing rounds.

'''Winner:''' [[Fredrikstad FK 2|A non-reserve club won the group]] {{efn|Reserve teams were not eligible for promotion if their senior side played in the top two divisions and league rules prevented advancement beyond a certain level.}}

=== Group 2 ===
Group 2 included clubs from the Oslo area and surrounding districts. The group was notable for a strong attacking record by its top sides and several high-scoring matches during the summer portion of the campaign.

'''Winner:''' [[Ullensaker/Kisa IL|Ullensaker/Kisa]]

=== Group 3 ===
Group 3 contained many western and central Norwegian clubs. The title race in this section was decided by consistency over the full season rather than by a late surge.

'''Winner:''' [[Kristiansund BK|Kristiansund]]

=== Group 4 ===
Group 4 was made up primarily of teams from northern and central Norway. Travel distances were especially long in this group, reflecting the geographical spread typical of lower-division football in Norway.

'''Winner:''' [[Tromsdalen UIL|Tromsdalen]]

== Season significance ==
The 2013 season was part of a period in which the Norwegian lower divisions continued to balance regional organization with national promotion and relegation. For ambitious clubs, the Second Division remained a key stepping stone toward professional football in the First Division and, ultimately, the top flight.

The season also highlighted the broad geographic reach of Norwegian football, with clubs from densely populated eastern districts as well as remote northern communities competing under the same national structure.
