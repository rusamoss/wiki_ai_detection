The 2013 Norwegian Second Division was the 2013 season of the Norwegian Second Division, the third tier of Norwegian football. The competition was contested by 56 teams divided into four groups of 14 teams.

Each team played the other teams in its group twice, once at home and once away, for a total of 26 matches. The winners of the four groups were promoted to the 2014 Norwegian First Division, while the lowest-placed teams in each group were relegated to the Third Division.

## Promotion

The following group winners were promoted to the 2014 Norwegian First Division:

| Group | Champions | Promoted to |
|---|---|---|
| Group 1 | Bærum | 2014 Norwegian First Division |
| Group 2 | Nest-Sotra | 2014 Norwegian First Division |
| Group 3 | Kristiansund | 2014 Norwegian First Division |
| Group 4 | Jerv | 2014 Norwegian First Division |

## Competition format

The league was organised geographically into four sections. Clubs received three points for a win, one point for a draw and no points for a defeat. If teams finished level on points, their positions were determined by goal difference and then by goals scored.

Reserve sides from clubs in the top two divisions were eligible to participate in the Second Division, but could not be promoted to the First Division if their senior team played at that level or higher.

## Groups

### Group 1

Bærum won Group 1 and secured promotion to the First Division.

### Group 2

Nest-Sotra won Group 2 and earned promotion to the First Division.

### Group 3

Kristiansund won Group 3 and were promoted to the First Division.

### Group 4

Jerv won Group 4 and completed the list of promoted teams.
