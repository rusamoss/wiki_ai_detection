'''Abraham Van Helsing''' is a fictional character in [[Bram Stoker]]'s 1897 Gothic horror novel ''[[Dracula (novel)|Dracula]]''. A Dutch physician and professor, he is called to England by his former student Dr. John Seward to help treat Lucy Westenra, whose unexplained illness is later revealed to be the result of attacks by the vampire Count Dracula. Van Helsing becomes the principal organizer of the group that identifies, pursues, and ultimately destroys Dracula.

In the novel, Van Helsing combines modern medical knowledge with familiarity with folklore, religion, and supernatural threats. He is portrayed as an experienced scholar and practical investigator whose willingness to accept apparently impossible evidence distinguishes him from the other characters. The name “Van Helsing” has since become closely associated with vampire hunting in popular culture.

==Role in ''Dracula''==

Van Helsing is introduced through Dr. John Seward, who had studied under him in Amsterdam and regards him as a mentor. Seward summons Van Helsing after Lucy Westenra suffers from recurring blood loss, weakness, and strange marks on her throat. Although Van Helsing initially withholds his conclusions, he recognizes that Lucy is being preyed upon by a vampire.

He attempts to protect Lucy using blood transfusions, garlic flowers, and other precautions. These measures are undermined by Lucy's mother, who removes the garlic from her daughter's room without understanding its purpose. Lucy dies and subsequently rises as an undead creature who attacks children near her home.

Van Helsing persuades Lucy's fiancé, Arthur Holmwood, along with Seward and Quincey Morris, to confront the undead Lucy. The group destroys her vampire form in a scene that establishes both Van Helsing's authority and the novel's moral framework: the act is presented as a painful but merciful release of Lucy's soul.

Afterward, Van Helsing helps assemble evidence against Count Dracula. He studies the Count's movements, explains the vampire's limitations, and directs efforts to locate and purify Dracula's hiding places in London. He also protects Mina Harker after Dracula begins targeting her. Van Helsing travels with Mina and the other pursuers toward Transylvania, where the group intercepts Dracula before he can return to his castle. While Jonathan Harker and Quincey Morris destroy Dracula, Van Helsing and Mina enter the castle and kill the vampire women who live there.

==Characterization==

Van Helsing is presented as an accomplished but unconventional physician. He values scientific methods but rejects the assumption that science can explain every phenomenon. His knowledge of vampirism is rooted in folklore and religious tradition, yet he approaches the subject with the seriousness of a medical specialist.

The character often speaks in grammatically unusual English, reflecting that English is not his first language. His speech distinguishes him from the English narrators and contributes to his image as an outsider who brings specialized knowledge unavailable to the other characters.

Although Van Helsing gives orders and interprets evidence for the group, he is not portrayed as unfeeling. He is deeply affected by Lucy's death and by the dangers faced by Mina Harker. His role combines that of physician, teacher, spiritual adviser, and military strategist.

==Themes==

Van Helsing is central to ''Dracula''{{'}}s treatment of the relationship between modernity and tradition. The novel includes contemporary technologies such as blood transfusions, phonographs, typewriters, and railway timetables, but these tools alone do not defeat Dracula. Van Helsing argues that scientific knowledge must be supplemented by openness to older beliefs and practices.

The character also represents collective action against Dracula. While several characters contribute important skills, Van Helsing provides the framework through which their observations become a coherent campaign against the Count. His leadership turns individual fear and confusion into coordinated resistance.

==In adaptations and popular culture==

Van Helsing has appeared in numerous adaptations of ''Dracula'', often with significant changes to his age, nationality, personality, and role. Some versions retain him as an elderly professor and vampire expert, while others reimagine him as an action-oriented hunter or a member of an organization devoted to combating supernatural creatures.

Notable portrayals include:

* [[Edward Van Sloan]] in the 1931 film ''[[Dracula (1931 English-language film)|Dracula]]'', where the character is named Professor Van Helsing.
* [[Peter Cushing]] in Hammer Film Productions' ''[[Horror of Dracula]]'' (1958) and several later Hammer Dracula films.
* [[Laurence Olivier]] in the 1979 film ''[[Dracula (1979 film)|Dracula]]''.
* [[Anthony Hopkins]] in Francis Ford Coppola's 1992 film ''[[Bram Stoker's Dracula (1992 film)|Bram Stoker's Dracula]]''.
* [[Hugh Jackman]] in the 2004 film ''[[Van Helsing (film)|Van Helsing]]'', which transforms the character into a younger monster hunter and expands his role beyond the events of Stoker's novel.

The surname “Van Helsing” has also been used for characters and institutions in later vampire fiction, including descendants, successors, and unrelated hunters. Such uses often draw on the character's reputation as an expert on vampires rather than on the details of Stoker's portrayal.
