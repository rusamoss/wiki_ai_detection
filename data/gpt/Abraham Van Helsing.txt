Abraham Van Helsing is a fictional character in Bram Stoker's 1897 Gothic horror novel Dracula. A Dutch physician, professor, and scholar of obscure diseases and folklore, he is summoned to England to assist in the treatment of Lucy Westenra, whose unexplained illness proves to be the result of attacks by Count Dracula. Van Helsing becomes the principal organizer of the group that investigates Dracula and ultimately destroys him.

Van Helsing is commonly depicted as an elderly but vigorous man with wide-ranging medical, scientific, and folkloric knowledge. His combination of rational inquiry and willingness to accept supernatural explanations distinguishes him from the other principal characters in the novel.

## Role in Dracula

Van Helsing is introduced through Dr. John Seward, a former student who requests his help with Lucy Westenra's deteriorating health. After examining Lucy, Van Helsing recognizes signs associated with vampirism, although he initially withholds his conclusions from Seward and the other men involved in her care. He attempts to protect Lucy by arranging blood transfusions and placing garlic flowers around her room.

Lucy dies despite these efforts and later rises as a vampire, preying upon children in the area. Van Helsing persuades Lucy's fiancé, Arthur Holmwood, along with Seward and Quincey Morris, to confront her. The group enters her tomb and destroys her undead form, allowing her to rest in peace.

Van Helsing subsequently explains the nature of Dracula to the others and leads the effort to locate and destroy the Count. He instructs the group in the use of crucifixes, consecrated wafers, garlic, and other protective measures. He also studies Dracula's movements through letters, diaries, newspaper reports, and other documents collected by the novel's narrators.

During the pursuit of Dracula, Van Helsing travels with Mina Harker to Transylvania. There he protects her from the vampire women inhabiting Dracula's castle and destroys them in their resting places. The other members of the group intercept Dracula while he is being transported back to his castle. Dracula is killed by Jonathan Harker and Quincey Morris, while Van Helsing's preparations prevent the Count from returning to safety.

## Characterization

Van Helsing is portrayed as both a man of science and a believer in ancient traditions. He uses contemporary medical methods, including blood transfusion, hypnosis, and psychiatric observation, but he also accepts the existence of vampires, sacred objects, and supernatural evil. In the novel, his expertise is presented as the result of extensive study across several fields rather than as simple superstition.

His speech is marked by non-native English grammar and unusual phrasing. This characteristic has been interpreted in adaptations in various ways, sometimes emphasizing his Dutch identity and sometimes replacing it with a more generalized European accent. Despite his occasional eccentricity, he is depicted as compassionate, determined, and deeply religious.

Van Helsing also serves as a mentor to the younger men in the novel. He gradually leads Seward, Holmwood, Harker, and Morris from skepticism to acceptance of the supernatural threat they face. His authority depends not only on his professional reputation but on his ability to reconcile evidence, practical action, and moral conviction.

## Influence and adaptations

Van Helsing has become one of the best-known vampire hunters in popular culture. In many later adaptations of Dracula, he is given a more active combat-oriented role than in Stoker's novel, where he is primarily a physician, investigator, and organizer.

The character has appeared in numerous films, television productions, radio dramas, comic books, video games, and novels. Actors who have portrayed Van Helsing include Edward Van Sloan in the 1931 film Dracula, Peter Cushing in Hammer Film Productions' vampire films, Laurence Olivier in the 1979 film Dracula, Anthony Hopkins in Bram Stoker's Dracula (1992), and Hugh Jackman in Van Helsing (2004).

Some adaptations expand Van Helsing into a member of a hereditary family of vampire hunters or place him in conflicts with other monsters. These interpretations differ substantially from Stoker's original character, whose central role is tied specifically to the investigation and destruction of Count Dracula.
