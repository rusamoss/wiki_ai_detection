Ahmed Abdisalam Adan (also spelled Ahmed Abdisalam Aden) is a Somali politician who served as Mayor of Mogadishu and Governor of the Banaadir administrative region during Somalia’s Transitional Federal Government period. He was appointed to the position in 2009, at a time when Mogadishu was affected by armed conflict between government forces and insurgent groups.

## Political career

Adan was appointed Mayor of Mogadishu and Governor of Banaadir in early 2009 by President Sheikh Sharif Sheikh Ahmed. The positions are commonly held together because Mogadishu is located within the Banaadir region and the mayor is responsible for both municipal administration and regional government functions.

His administration operated during a period of insecurity in the Somali capital. Much of Mogadishu was contested by forces aligned with the Transitional Federal Government, the African Union Mission in Somalia (AMISOM), and armed opposition groups. The city’s public services, infrastructure, and local administration had been severely weakened by years of civil war.

As mayor, Adan called for greater international and domestic support for the restoration of security and public services in Mogadishu. His tenure included efforts to re-establish municipal authority in government-controlled districts, although the administration’s reach was limited by continuing violence and political instability.

Adan left office in 2010. He was succeeded as Mayor of Mogadishu and Governor of Banaadir by Mohamed Ahmed Nur, commonly known as “Tarzan”.
