# Adegboyega Folaranmi Adedoyin

**Adegboyega Folaranmi Adedoyin** (11 September 1922 – 31 January 2014) was a Nigerian physician, high jumper, and long jumper whose athletic career bridged the colonial and post-colonial eras of Nigerian sport. Because Nigeria did not yet have a recognized National Olympic Committee, he represented Great Britain at the 1948 Summer Olympics in London, becoming the first Nigerian-born athlete to reach an Olympic final. After his sporting career, he returned to Nigeria, where he practised as an obstetrician and gynaecologist for several decades.

## Early life and education

Adedoyin was born in Sagamu (then Shagamu), in present-day Ogun State, Nigeria. He was a son of William Adedoyin, the Akarigbo (traditional ruler) of Remoland, making him a member of the Remo royal family. He grew up during the period of British colonial rule and later travelled to Northern Ireland to study medicine at Queen's University Belfast in the early 1940s. 

While at Queen's University, he became involved in athletics after being encouraged to join university training sessions. His natural ability in both the high jump and long jump quickly attracted attention in university and national competitions.

## Athletics career

Adedoyin competed for Queen's University Belfast Athletics Club and established himself among Britain's leading field athletes in the late 1940s. In 1947 he won the Amateur Athletic Association (AAA) high jump championship with a clearance of 1.93 metres, one of the most prestigious athletics titles in Britain at the time.

At the 1948 Summer Olympics in London, he entered both the men's high jump and men's long jump for Great Britain. His participation reflected the political circumstances of the period, as Nigeria had not yet formed an Olympic team recognized by the International Olympic Committee.

In the high jump, Adedoyin qualified for the final and ultimately finished twelfth. In the long jump he achieved a mark of 7.27 metres to place fifth, the highest Olympic finish by a Nigerian-born athlete at the time. His appearance in the Olympic finals marked an important milestone in the history of Nigerian athletics.

His recorded personal bests were:

* High jump: 1.969 m (1949)
* Long jump: 7.35 m (1947)

## Medical career

Following the Olympics, Adedoyin returned to Nigeria to pursue the medical profession. He specialised in obstetrics and gynaecology and practised medicine for many years, becoming better known professionally as a physician than as a former international athlete.

## Legacy

Adedoyin occupies a distinctive place in Nigerian sporting history as one of the country's earliest internationally successful athletes and the first Nigerian to compete in an Olympic final. His career illustrates the experience of colonial-era athletes who represented Britain before Nigeria established its own Olympic movement.

In later life he received recognition in Ogun State for his pioneering role in Nigerian athletics. He also remained notable as a member of the Remo royal family and has been identified as an uncle of Nigerian Olympic medal-winning sprinter Falilat Ogunkoya.

## Personal life

Adedoyin was born into the ruling house of Remoland. He balanced careers in elite athletics and medicine, ultimately devoting most of his life to clinical practice after retiring from competition. He died in Abeokuta, Ogun State, on 31 January 2014, aged 91.

## Selected achievements

* Winner of the 1947 AAA High Jump Championship.
* Competed for Great Britain at the 1948 Summer Olympics.
* First Nigerian-born athlete to qualify for an Olympic final.
* Fifth place in the men's long jump at the 1948 Olympic Games.
* Practised as an obstetrician and gynaecologist in Nigeria following his athletics career.
