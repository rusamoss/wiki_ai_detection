# 2013 Norwegian Second Division

The **2013 Norwegian Second Division** (Norwegian: *2. divisjon 2013*) was a season of the Norwegian Second Division, the third-highest tier of the Norwegian football league system, sitting below the Adeccoligaen (First Division) and above the Third Division. The season formed part of the broader Norwegian football calendar, running from spring to autumn in accordance with the traditional Scandinavian football calendar, which avoids the harsh winter months.

## Format

The 2013 Norwegian Second Division was contested by clubs organized into several geographically based groups, reflecting the regional structure typical of Norwegian lower-league football. This structure was designed to reduce travel distances for clubs, given Norway's long and sparsely populated geography, while still maintaining a nationally recognized third tier of competition.

Each group played a round-robin schedule, with clubs meeting each other twice—once at home and once away—over the course of the season. Points were awarded on the standard basis of three points for a win, one for a draw, and none for a loss, with final standings determined by total points, followed by goal difference and goals scored as tiebreakers where necessary.

## Promotion and relegation

The winners of each regional group in the Second Division earned the right to compete for promotion to the Norwegian First Division. Depending on the number of available promotion spots, some group winners were promoted directly, while others were required to compete in promotion play-off matches against clubs from neighboring groups or against lower-placed First Division clubs facing relegation.

At the other end of the table, clubs finishing in the lowest positions of their respective groups faced relegation to the Third Division, the fourth tier of Norwegian football. As with promotion, relegation was sometimes decided through play-off matches involving clubs from adjacent groups, ensuring competitive balance across the regional divisions.

## Structure of the league system

The Second Division occupied a significant position within the broader Norwegian football pyramid. Below the fully professional Eliteserien and the semi-professional First Division, the Second Division represented the highest level of largely amateur and semi-professional competition, serving as a proving ground for clubs and players aspiring to reach the professional ranks. Many clubs competing in the 2013 season fielded a mixture of experienced veteran players and promising youth prospects from regional academies.

## Clubs

Participating clubs in the 2013 season were drawn from across Norway, with groups typically representing distinct geographic regions such as the eastern, western, central, and northern parts of the country. Club rosters ranged from reserve or "second" teams of larger professional clubs to standalone semi-professional clubs representing smaller towns and communities.

## Significance

The 2013 season continued the Second Division's traditional role of feeding talent and promoted clubs into the higher tiers of Norwegian football, while also providing a competitive outlet for clubs relegated from the First Division. The season's results contributed to shaping the composition of the First Division for the following year, as well as determining which clubs would drop into regional Third Division competition.
