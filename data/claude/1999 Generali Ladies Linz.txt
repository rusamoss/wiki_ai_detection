# 1999 Generali Ladies Linz

The **1999 Generali Ladies Linz** was a women's tennis tournament played on indoor hard courts in Linz, Austria. It was the third edition of the Generali Ladies Linz tournament, part of the WTA Tour, and was held in October 1999 at the Design Center Linz. The event offered prize money in the mid-tier category of the women's tour calendar and attracted a field that combined leading Austrian players with international competitors from the WTA Tour's European indoor swing.

## Tournament Overview

As with previous editions, the Generali Ladies Linz was contested on an indoor hard court and served as one of the marquee domestic events for Austrian tennis. The tournament featured both singles and doubles competitions, drawing a field of top-ranked players eager for ranking points before the close of the season. Local favorite Barbara Schett, one of Austria's leading players at the time, was among the notable competitors, and Austrian interest in the tournament was consistently strong given the country's affinity for the event since its founding in the 1990s.

The singles draw featured a mix of seeded and unseeded players competing across the main draw, with qualifying rounds held in the days preceding the main tournament to fill out the field. The doubles competition ran concurrently, offering a separate title and prize allocation.

## Singles

The singles competition concluded with a straight-sets or hard-fought three-set final, reflecting the competitive depth of the WTA field that season. Barbara Schett, playing in front of a home crowd, was among the strongest contenders in the draw, continuing her strong record at the tournament in the late 1990s. The final matched two players who had progressed through a demanding draw featuring several seeded competitors from the WTA Tour's upper rankings.

## Doubles

The doubles event was contested alongside the singles tournament, with pairings drawn from both the singles competitors and specialists in doubles play. As was typical for tour events of this size, the doubles final featured a best-of-three-sets match, with the champions earning ranking points and prize money in addition to the trophy.

## Venue and Reception

The Design Center Linz, known for its distinctive glass-domed architecture, served as the host venue, providing an indoor setting well-suited for the October scheduling of the tournament amid the transition to the European winter indoor season. The event continued to be well attended by local fans, cementing Linz's reputation as a fixture on the WTA calendar following its introduction in 1997.

## Legacy

The 1999 edition contributed to the growing history of the Generali Ladies Linz tournament, which continued to be held annually in subsequent years and remained an established stop on the WTA Tour's European indoor circuit into the 21st century. The tournament's continued sponsorship by Generali, an international insurance company, helped secure its place on the calendar for years following the 1999 edition.
