# Adegboyega Folaranmi Adedoyin

**Prince Adegboyega Folaranmi Adedoyin** (11 September 1922 – January 2014) was a Nigerian-born physician and athlete widely credited as Nigeria's first Olympian, having competed for Great Britain at the 1948 London Olympic Games. He was an Ijebu Remo prince and medical student at Queen's University Belfast at the time.

## Early life

Adedoyin was born in 1922, the son of the Akarigbo (king) of Ijebu Remo in southern Nigeria. He travelled to Belfast to study medicine in 1942, reportedly on the advice of his brother "Zik," who was studying in London at the time. Soon after arriving in Northern Ireland by boat, he became involved in local sport, almost by accident — a university coach invited him to join in while he was watching a match, and he quickly showed natural ability. An early Irish newspaper report from May 1946 described him as a tall West African athlete with obvious promise, particularly in the high jump.

## Athletic career

Adedoyin went on to set several records during his student years: he broke the Queen's University high jump record in 1945, and the following year broke the Universities Athletic Union championship record with a clearance of 6 feet 1 inch. In 1947 he won the AAA Championships with a high jump of 1.93 metres.

Adedoyin competed for Great Britain at the 1948 Olympic Games because Nigeria did not yet have recognition from the International Olympic Committee. He reached the final of the long jump at those Games, and by some accounts also produced the best results of anyone on the British team across his events. He finished fifth in the long jump final. While in Britain he also won several meets in the high jump and long jump at combined university competitions.

## Later life

After his studies, Adedoyin returned to Nigeria to practise obstetrics and gynaecology. He married Hannah Hotoba-During, a Sierra Leonean woman who had grown up in Belfast, after graduating in 1949; the couple then moved to Liverpool before relocating first to Sierra Leone and later to Nigeria. In September 2012, the Ogun State Government honoured Adedoyin as Nigeria's first Olympian, recognizing his descent from one of the ruling houses of Remoland.

He died in January 2014.
