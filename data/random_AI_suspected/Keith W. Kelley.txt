Keith W. Kelley (born November 5, 1947) is an American immunophysiologist, researcher and academic. He is professor emeritus of Immunophysiology at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign.His research has focused on interactions between the immune, nervous and endocrine systems, particularly in the fields of psychoneuroimmunology and immunopsychiatry.

He served as the second editor-in-chief of the journal Brain, Behavior, and Immunity from 2003 to 2018. In 2011, he was elected a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS). In 2023 he also became the founding Managing Associate Editor of Brain, Behavior, and Immunity Integrative, a journal.

## Early life and education

Kelley was born in Bloomington, Illinois, on November 5, 1947. He received a Bachelor of Science degree from Illinois State University in 1969. Following graduation, he was drafted into the United States Army and served for fourteen months with a field artillery fire direction control unit near Nha Trang during the Vietnam War.

After completing military service in 1971, Kelley enrolled at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign, where he earned a Master of Science degree in 1973 and a Doctor of Philosophy degree in 1976. His doctoral training combined immunology, physiology and endocrinology, forming the basis for his later research on communication between the immune and nervous systems.

## Career

Following completion of his doctoral studies in 1976, Kelley joined the faculty of the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign, where he established a research program in immunophysiology. His research received support from agencies including the National Institutes of Health, the United States Department of Agriculture, and the National Science Foundation. In 2003, Kelley succeeded Robert Ader as the second editor-in-chief of Brain, Behavior, and Immunity, the official journal of the Psychoneuroimmunology Research Society.

Kelley also served as Secretary-Treasurer of the Psychoneuroimmunology Research Society and also contributed to the creation of PNIRS Asia-Pacific and PNIRS Ibero-America, facilitating collaboration among researchers throughout Asia and Latin America.

Kelley retired from the University of Illinois in 2011 and was subsequently named professor emeritus. In 2021, he was appointed as a visiting scholar in the School of Psychological Sciences at Shenzhen University, China and in 2023 as a Global Faculty Affiliate Professor in the College of Health Sciences at VinUniversity in Vietnam.

## Research

Kelley's research has focused on understanding the bidirectional communication between the immune system and the central nervous system. During the early stages of his career, the immune system was generally viewed as functioning independently from the brain except in pathological conditions. Through laboratory and translational studies, Kelley helped demonstrate that the nervous, endocrine and immune systems communicate continuously through hormones, cytokines and neural pathways.

One area of Kelley's research examined the influence of stress hormones, including glucocorticoids and catecholamines, on immune regulation. His work showed that neuroendocrine responses to physiological stress alter immune-cell activity and inflammatory processes, providing evidence that the central nervous system regulates immune responses through endocrine signaling.

Another major contribution involved demonstrating that cytokines released during immune activation act on the brain to produce coordinated behavioral and physiological responses collectively known as sickness behavior. These responses include fatigue, decreased appetite, reduced social interaction, sleep alterations and diminished physical activity. The concept has become central to research on infection, chronic inflammatory disease and psychiatric illness.

Kelley also investigated interactions between inflammatory cytokines and insulin-like growth factor I (IGF-I), demonstrating how inflammatory mediators interfere with anabolic signaling pathways involved in growth, tissue repair and metabolism.These findings contributed to understanding the mechanisms responsible for muscle wasting, impaired growth and metabolic dysfunction associated with chronic inflammatory diseases.

His later work examined neuroimmune mechanisms underlying depression, neurodegeneration and other psychiatric disorders. Reviews co-authored by Kelley synthesized evidence linking chronic inflammation with behavioral and cognitive disorders and have become among the most highly cited publications in psychoneuroimmunology.

In addition to experimental research, Kelley has written historical and conceptual analyses documenting the emergence of psychoneuroimmunology and its evolution into the interdisciplinary field of immuno-psychiatry.

## Editorial work

Kelley has played a role in the development of scientific publishing in the fields of psychoneuroimmunology and immunopsychiatry. In 2003, he succeeded Robert Ader as the second editor-in-chief of Brain, Behavior, and Immunity, the official journal of the Psychoneuroimmunology Research Society (PNIRS). He served in that position until 2017, overseeing the journal during a period of expansion in both submissions and international readership.

During his tenure, the journal broadened its editorial scope to include research in neuroscience, immunology, psychiatry, psychology and behavioral medicine, reflecting the increasing interdisciplinary nature of psychoneuroimmunology. Kelley also encouraged publication of translational and clinical research examining the role of inflammation in neurological and psychiatric disorders.

In 2023, Kelley became the founding Managing Associate Editor of Brain, Behavior, and Immunity Integrative, an Elsevier journal established to publish research on neuroimmune interactions, integrative medicine and interdisciplinary approaches to health with an emphasis on Asia medicine. The journal complements Brain, Behavior, and Immunity by emphasizing integrative physiological and clinical research.

He has also contributed to the organization of international conferences and educational initiatives promoting collaboration among researchers in immunology, neuroscience and behavioral medicine.
