Sawai Madhopur Bypass Line Project is a railway infrastructure project under construction near Sawai Madhopur Junction in the Indian state of Rajasthan. The project involves the construction of a 13.54 km (8.41 mi) double-track railway bypass between Devpura railway station and Kushtala railway station, together with 25 kV AC overhead electrification. The project is being implemented by Indian Railways through the North Western Railway.

The bypass is intended to allow trains travelling between Kota and Jaipur to avoid reversing direction at Sawai Madhopur Junction. It is expected to improve train operations, reduce congestion at the junction and increase the capacity of the railway network in the area.

The Sawai Madhopur Bypass Line Project forms part of the ongoing capacity enhancement works on the Kota–Jaipur main line. After completion, trains will be able to use either the existing route through Sawai Madhopur Junction or the bypass line, depending on operational requirements.

History

The Sawai Madhopur Bypass Line Project was proposed by Indian Railways to improve train operations on the Kota–Jaipur route near Sawai Madhopur Junction. The project was planned to provide an alternative alignment for through trains that do not require a halt at the junction.

The project includes the construction of a 13.54 km (8.41 mi) double-track railway bypass between Devpura railway station and Kushtala railway station, along with 25 kV AC overhead electrification. It forms part of the ongoing railway capacity enhancement works on the Kota–Jaipur main line.

The project has been included in the planning documents of the North Western Railway and the India Investment Grid. Preliminary activities, including land acquisition and other preparatory works, were initiated before the start of major construction.

After completion, the bypass is expected to provide an additional route for trains travelling between Kota and Jaipur without entering Sawai Madhopur Junction, improving operational flexibility on the corridor.

Route

The Sawai Madhopur Bypass Line Project extends from Devpura railway station to Kushtala railway station near Sawai Madhopur Junction in the Indian state of Rajasthan. The proposed bypass is approximately 13.54 km (8.41 mi) long and is being constructed as a double-track railway line with 25 kV AC overhead electrification.

The bypass provides an alternative alignment to the existing route through Sawai Madhopur Junction. It is intended for through trains travelling between Kota and Jaipur that do not require a scheduled halt at the junction. Trains requiring a commercial stop at Sawai Madhopur Junction will continue to use the existing route.

The alignment reconnects with the existing railway network near Kushtala railway station, allowing trains to continue towards Kota or Jaipur without reversing direction at Sawai Madhopur Junction. The project also includes the construction of railway bridges, earthworks, signalling infrastructure and overhead electrification along the bypass.

The project also includes 25 kV AC overhead electrification, railway bridges, earthworks, track laying, signalling and telecommunications infrastructure.

Purpose and significance

The bypass is expected to reduce the need for through trains to reverse direction at the junction, helping to simplify train movements and improve operational efficiency.

The project is also expected to increase the capacity of the railway network by separating through traffic from trains that originate, terminate or have scheduled halts at Sawai Madhopur Junction.
